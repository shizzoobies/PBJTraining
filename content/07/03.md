# Recurring Transactions

Recurring transactions let you automate the routine entries you make every month — saving time, reducing errors, and making sure nothing gets missed.

## What You'll Learn

- Common scenarios where recurring transactions are most useful

## What Are Recurring Transactions?

A **recurring transaction** is a saved template that QuickBooks Online uses to automatically create — or remind you to create — a transaction on a regular schedule.

If you pay the same rent every month, send the same retainer invoice to a client every month, or record a monthly depreciation journal entry, you are doing repetitive work that QuickBooks can handle for you.

Recurring transactions are one of the most underused time-savers in QuickBooks Online. Once set up, they run in the background without any effort on your part — or they prompt you at the right time so you never forget.

## Transaction Types That Can Recur

Not every transaction type supports recurring templates, but the most common ones do. Here is what you can set to recur in QuickBooks Online:

- **Invoices** — Ideal for monthly retainers, subscriptions, or regular service fees
- **Expenses** — Great for recurring software subscriptions charged to a business card you enter manually
- **Bills** — For regular vendor bills like rent, utilities, or monthly service contracts
- **Checks** — For regular payments you write manually
- **Journal Entries** — Essential for monthly depreciation, prepaid expense amortization, or accruals
- **Credit Card Credits** — For expected monthly credits from vendors
- **Purchase Orders** — For regular supply orders
- **Sales Receipts** — For regular cash transactions

## The Three Types of Recurring Templates

When you create a recurring template, you choose one of three automation levels:

**Scheduled** — QuickBooks creates the transaction automatically on the date you specify, with no action required from you. Use this when the amount and details never change (e.g., a $1,500/month rent expense).

**Reminder** — QuickBooks sends you a reminder notification that a recurring transaction is due, but you must review and approve it before it posts. Use this when the amount might vary or you want a chance to adjust details before it goes through.

**Unscheduled** — QuickBooks saves the template but never triggers it automatically. You manually go in and use the template when you need it. Use this for transactions that happen irregularly but follow the same format each time.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Start with Reminder type for any recurring template where the amount could change — like a monthly utility bill that fluctuates. Once you are confident the amount is always the same, you can switch it to Scheduled. It is much easier to correct a missed posting than to fix an incorrect one that auto-posted unnoticed.

## Creating a Recurring Template

Here is the step-by-step process for creating a new recurring transaction from scratch:

1. Click the **Gear icon** (Settings) in the upper-right corner of QuickBooks Online.
2. Under the "Lists" column, click **Recurring Transactions**.
3. Click the **New** button in the upper-right corner.
4. A dialog box will appear asking you to choose a transaction type. Select the type you want (e.g., Invoice, Bill, Expense, Journal Entry) and click **OK**.
5. The transaction form opens with a recurring setup panel at the top. In the **Template name** field, enter a descriptive name (e.g., "Monthly Office Rent – Main Street" or "Retainer Invoice – Acme Corp").
6. In the **Type** field, choose **Scheduled**, **Reminder**, or **Unscheduled**.
7. If you chose Scheduled or Reminder, set the **Interval**: Daily, Weekly, Monthly, Yearly, or a custom interval.
8. Set the **Start date** — the date the first instance should be created or triggered.
9. Set the **End** option: Never, By (specific end date), or After a certain number of occurrences.
10. Fill in the rest of the transaction form exactly as you would for a normal transaction — customer or vendor, account, amount, description, and any class or location.
11. Click **Save Template**.

The template now appears in your Recurring Transactions list and will run according to the schedule you set.

## Managing Existing Recurring Templates

Your Recurring Transactions list (Gear icon > Recurring Transactions) is your control center for all templates. Here is what you can do from that list:

**To edit a template:**
1. Find the template in the list.
2. Click the dropdown arrow under the "Action" column and select **Edit**.
3. Make your changes and click **Save Template**.
4. Note: Editing a template does not retroactively change transactions that have already been created. It only affects future instances.

**To use an Unscheduled template:**
1. Find the unscheduled template in the list.
2. Click **Use** under the Action column.
3. A transaction form opens with all the template details pre-filled. Review, adjust if needed, and save.

**To pause a recurring template:**
1. Click the dropdown under Action and select **Edit**.
2. Change the **Type** from Scheduled to **Unscheduled**, or change the end date to today.
3. Save the template. This stops future auto-creation without deleting the template.

**To delete a recurring template:**
1. Click the dropdown under Action and select **Delete**.
2. Confirm the deletion.
3. Deleting the template does not delete transactions that were already created from it. Those remain in your books.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Review your Recurring Transactions list every quarter. Templates for vendors you no longer use, clients whose retainers ended, or one-time setups that were never removed can silently clutter your books for months. A quarterly cleanup takes five minutes and prevents hard-to-find errors.

## Real-World Use Cases

**Monthly retainer invoices:** A marketing agency charges five clients a fixed $2,000/month retainer. Instead of manually creating five invoices each month, they set up five Scheduled recurring invoices. On the first of each month, all five invoices are created automatically and ready to send.

**Rent and lease payments:** A business owner pays $3,800/month in office rent. They set up a Scheduled recurring bill from their landlord. On the due date, the bill appears automatically and just needs to be paid.

**Monthly depreciation:** An accountant sets up a Scheduled recurring journal entry to record $250/month in equipment depreciation. This ensures the books are accurate each month without manual intervention.

**Utility bills with variable amounts:** A restaurant sets up a Reminder recurring bill for their monthly electricity bill. On the 15th of each month, they receive a reminder, enter the actual amount from the bill, and post it.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Setting recurring invoices to Scheduled when amounts change.** If a client's monthly charge varies, use Reminder instead. Scheduled invoices post automatically — if the amount is wrong, you may not catch it until the client complains.
- **Forgetting to set an end date.** A template for a project-based client should have an end date. Without one, invoices will keep generating long after the engagement ended.
- **Not naming templates descriptively.** A template named "Monthly Invoice" is useless when you have 20 of them. Use names like "Retainer – Acme Corp – $2000/month."
- **Assuming Scheduled means paid.** A Scheduled recurring bill creates the bill in QuickBooks — it does not actually pay it. You still need to process the payment separately unless you have bank syncing set up.
- **Deleting instead of pausing.** If a recurring transaction is temporarily on hold (like a seasonal service), pause it rather than delete it. Deleting means rebuilding from scratch when the service resumes.

## Key Takeaways

- Recurring transactions eliminate manual data entry for any transaction that happens on a predictable schedule.
- The three template types — Scheduled, Reminder, and Unscheduled — give you control over how much automation you want.
- You can create recurring templates for invoices, expenses, bills, journal entries, and more.
- Manage all templates from Gear icon > Recurring Transactions, where you can edit, pause, or delete them at any time.
- Deleting a template does not remove transactions already created from it — and editing a template only affects future instances.
