# Classes and Locations for Tracking

QuickBooks Online's Classes and Locations features let you slice your financial data by department, profit center, or physical location — giving you P&L reports that go far beyond a single bottom line.

## What You'll Learn
- What Classes and Locations are and how they differ
- How to enable both features in QuickBooks Online settings
- How to assign a class or location to invoices, expenses, and other transactions
- How to run a Profit & Loss by Class and P&L by Location report
- Real-world use cases for small businesses with multiple revenue streams
- Limitations to be aware of before you rely on this data

---

## What Are Classes and Locations?

Think of **Classes** and **Locations** as two different lenses for viewing your business finances.

A **Class** typically represents a department, revenue stream, or profit center — something internal to how your business is organized. Examples include:
- Service vs. Retail sales
- Marketing Department vs. Operations
- Residential clients vs. Commercial clients

A **Location** typically represents a physical place or distinct business unit. Examples include:
- Downtown Store vs. Northside Store
- Warehouse vs. Office
- City A vs. City B

The key difference: you can assign one Class and one Location to any given transaction. They work independently, so you can report on either dimension — or both at once.

## Enabling Classes and Locations in Settings

Before you can use these features, you need to turn them on. They are off by default in all QuickBooks Online plans (Simple Start, Essentials, Plus, and Advanced).

**To enable Classes and Locations:**

1. Click the **Gear icon** (Settings) in the upper-right corner of QuickBooks Online.
2. Under the "Your Company" column, click **Account and Settings**.
3. Click the **Advanced** tab on the left side of the settings panel.
4. Scroll down to find the **Categories** section.
5. Click the pencil (Edit) icon next to Categories.
6. Toggle **Track classes** to ON. A sub-option will appear asking if you want to warn users when a transaction is saved without a class — enable this if you want stricter tracking.
7. Toggle **Track locations** to ON.
8. In the "Location label" dropdown, choose the label that best fits your business: Location, Business, Department, Division, Property, Store, or Territory.
9. Click **Save**, then click **Done**.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Choosing the right location label matters for report readability. If you run a retail chain, choose "Store." If you run a service firm with regional teams, choose "Territory" or "Division." This label appears on all your reports and transaction forms.

## Setting Up Your Class and Location Lists

Once enabled, you need to create the actual Classes and Locations you'll assign to transactions.

**To create Classes:**
1. Click the **Gear icon** > **All Lists**.
2. Click **Classes**.
3. Click **New** in the upper right.
4. Enter a name (e.g., "Residential Services") and click **Save**.
5. Repeat for each class you need.

**To create Locations:**
1. Click the **Gear icon** > **All Lists**.
2. Click **Locations**.
3. Click **New** in the upper right.
4. Enter a name (e.g., "Downtown Store") and click **Save**.
5. Repeat for each location.

You can also create sub-classes or sub-locations (parent/child hierarchy) for more granular reporting. For example, a parent class of "Services" with sub-classes of "Plumbing" and "Electrical."

## Assigning Classes and Locations to Transactions

Once your lists are set up, a **Class** column and **Location** field will appear on transaction forms throughout QuickBooks Online. Here is how to use them:

**On an Invoice:**
- Open or create an invoice (+ New > Invoice).
- The **Location** field appears near the top of the form, next to the customer name.
- The **Class** column appears on each line item — you can assign different classes to different line items on the same invoice.

**On an Expense:**
- Open or create an expense (+ New > Expense).
- The **Location** field appears near the top.
- The **Class** column appears on each line item.

**On a Bill:**
- Same layout as expenses — Location at the top, Class per line item.

**On a Check:**
- Same as above.

**On a Journal Entry:**
- Class and Location appear as columns on each debit/credit line.

[IMAGE: QuickBooks Online invoice form showing the Class column on line items and Location field at the top]

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** If you enabled the "warn when no class" option in settings, QuickBooks will prompt you before saving any transaction that is missing a class assignment. This is highly recommended when you need complete data for P&L by Class reports — one uncategorized transaction can skew your results.

## Running P&L by Class and P&L by Location Reports

The real payoff comes in your reports. With classes and locations assigned, you can generate separate P&L statements for each segment of your business.

**To run a P&L by Class report:**
1. Click **Reports** in the left navigation menu.
2. In the search bar, type "Profit and Loss by Class" and click the report when it appears.
3. Set your desired date range at the top.
4. Click **Run Report**.
5. The report displays one column per class, with totals on the right — letting you compare profitability across departments or revenue streams side by side.

**To run a P&L by Location report:**
1. Click **Reports** in the left navigation menu.
2. Search for "Profit and Loss by Location."
3. Set your date range and click **Run Report**.
4. Each location appears as its own column.

You can also filter a standard P&L report by a single class or location using the filter options at the top of any report.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Not assigning classes or locations consistently.** If even 20% of your transactions lack a class, your P&L by Class report will have a large "Unclassified" column that makes the data unreliable. Build the habit of assigning these on every transaction from day one.
- **Using too many classes.** More than 10-15 classes creates cluttered reports that are hard to interpret. Keep your list focused on meaningful business segments.
- **Confusing classes with chart of accounts.** Classes and locations don't replace your account structure — they add a layer on top of it. Don't create a class for every income account you already have.
- **Forgetting to assign classes on payroll.** If you run payroll through QBO, you may need to manually map payroll expenses to classes — this does not happen automatically in all setups.
- **Mixing up Classes and Locations.** Decide upfront what each dimension represents and document your rules. Inconsistent use (e.g., sometimes using a location as a class) makes reports meaningless.

## Practice Exercise

Your business has two revenue streams: "Consulting" and "Training." You also operate from two offices: "Main Office" and "Remote."

1. Enable Classes and Locations in Account and Settings > Advanced.
2. Create two classes: Consulting and Training.
3. Create two locations: Main Office and Remote.
4. Create a sample invoice for $2,500 to a test customer, with line items assigned to the Consulting class and the Main Office location.
5. Create a sample expense for $500 (e.g., software subscription), assign it to the Training class and the Remote location.
6. Run a P&L by Class report and verify that income and expenses appear under the correct class columns.

## Key Takeaways

- Classes and Locations are separate dimensions — Classes track what type of work or department, Locations track where the work happens.
- Both must be enabled in Account and Settings > Advanced before they appear on transaction forms.
- Consistent assignment is critical — partial data produces misleading reports.
- The P&L by Class and P&L by Location reports let you compare profitability across business segments without maintaining separate QuickBooks files.
- This feature is available on all QBO plans, but is most valuable for businesses with multiple revenue streams, departments, or physical locations.
