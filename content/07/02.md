# Projects and Job Costing

QuickBooks Online's Projects feature lets you group all income, expenses, and time related to a specific job in one place — so you always know whether a project is making money or bleeding it.

## What You'll Learn
- What Projects are in QuickBooks Online and how they differ from customers
- How to enable the Projects feature in your settings
- How to create a project and link it to a customer
- How to add invoices, expenses, and time entries to a project
- How to read the Project Profitability report
- Which types of businesses benefit most from job costing

---

## What Are Projects in QuickBooks Online?

A **Project** is a container that holds all the financial activity related to a single job. Instead of hunting through transactions to figure out what you spent and earned on a particular job, QuickBooks bundles everything together for you.

Projects are built on top of your existing Customer records. Every project must be linked to a customer — think of the customer as "who hired you" and the project as "for what specific job."

For example:
- Customer: Riverside Construction Co.
- Project: Office Renovation — Phase 2

This setup lets you track multiple jobs for the same client independently. If Riverside hires you for three different projects this year, each one gets its own profitability snapshot.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Projects require a QuickBooks Online **Plus** or **Advanced** subscription. If you are on Simple Start or Essentials, you will need to upgrade to access this feature.

## Enabling Projects

Projects are not turned on by default. Here is how to enable the feature:

1. Click the **Gear icon** (Settings) in the upper-right corner.
2. Under "Your Company," click **Account and Settings**.
3. Click the **Advanced** tab.
4. Scroll to the **Projects** section.
5. Click the pencil (Edit) icon.
6. Toggle **Organize all job-related activity in one place** to ON.
7. Click **Save**, then click **Done**.

After saving, a **Projects** option will appear in your left navigation menu.

## Creating a Project and Linking It to a Customer

Once Projects is enabled, setting up your first project takes about two minutes.

**To create a project:**

1. Click **Projects** in the left navigation menu.
2. Click the green **New Project** button in the upper right.
3. In the "Project name" field, enter a descriptive name (e.g., "Kitchen Remodel – 123 Oak Street").
4. In the "Customer" dropdown, select the customer this project belongs to. If the customer does not exist yet, you can add them from this screen.
5. Optionally, enter a **Status** (In Progress, Completed, or Cancelled) and any notes.
6. Click **Save**.

The project now appears in your Projects list. You can click on it at any time to see all related financial activity and the profitability summary.

## Adding Transactions to a Project

The real power of Projects comes from consistently linking all related transactions. Here is how to connect each transaction type:

**Invoices:**
1. Go to **+ New > Invoice**.
2. In the "Customer" field, select the customer.
3. A second dropdown will appear below the customer name — select the **project** from this dropdown.
4. Fill in the invoice details and save.

**Expenses:**
1. Go to **+ New > Expense**.
2. In the "Payee" field, enter the vendor.
3. In the "Customer/Project" column on each line item, select the project name.
4. Make sure the **Billable** checkbox is checked if you plan to pass this cost to the client.
5. Save the expense.

**Bills:**
1. Go to **+ New > Bill**.
2. On each line item, use the "Customer/Project" column to link the bill to a project.
3. Check "Billable" if applicable.

**Time Entries:**
1. Go to **+ New > Single Time Activity** (or use Timesheets).
2. In the "Customer/Project" field, select the project.
3. Check the **Billable** checkbox if the time will be invoiced to the client.
4. Enter hours worked and the service item.
5. Save.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Any expense or time entry marked as Billable will appear as a line item you can add to the client's next invoice with one click. This ensures you never forget to bill for a reimbursable cost.

## Reading the Project Profitability Report

The Project Profitability report is the centerpiece of job costing. Here is how to access it and what it tells you:

1. Click **Projects** in the left navigation menu.
2. Click on any project to open its detail view.
3. At the top, you will see a summary card showing:
   - **Income** — total invoiced to the client for this project
   - **Expenses** — total costs logged to this project
   - **Profit** — Income minus Expenses
   - **Profit margin** — expressed as a percentage
4. Scroll down to see a full transaction list broken into Income and Expenses tabs.
5. For a broader view across all projects, click **Reports** > search for **"Project Profitability"** to see every active project ranked by profitability.

**Tracking Actual vs. Estimated Costs:**

If you know your budget going in, you can add estimated revenue and costs to a project:
1. Open a project and click **Add estimate** (or link an existing Estimate transaction).
2. QuickBooks will display a progress bar showing how much of the estimated budget has been used.
3. This "budget vs. actual" view is one of the most useful tools for contractors who bid fixed-price jobs.

## Who Benefits Most from Projects?

Projects and job costing are not necessary for every business — but they are transformative for the right ones:

**General contractors and subcontractors** — Track labor, materials, and subcontractor costs per job to see if your bids are accurate.

**Consultants and agencies** — Know whether each client engagement is profitable after accounting for all staff hours and expenses.

**Event planners and photographers** — Group vendor payments and client invoices by event to see your true margin per booking.

**IT service businesses** — Track time and materials for each client project separately from ongoing managed service work.

**Landscaping and home service companies** — Compare profitability across residential and commercial jobs to prioritize your best work.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Forgetting to link expenses to a project.** If you pay a subcontractor but don't assign the bill to a project, the cost disappears from your job costing report. Make it a habit to check the Customer/Project field on every expense.
- **Not marking billable expenses as billable.** Unchecking the Billable box means the cost won't appear on the client's next invoice — you'll absorb it instead of passing it through.
- **Using one project for all work from a customer.** If a client hires you for multiple distinct jobs, give each one its own project. Combining them makes profitability data meaningless.
- **Ignoring time tracking.** For service businesses, labor is often the biggest cost. If your team's time is not logged to a project, you are only seeing part of the picture.
- **Never running the Project Profitability report.** Setting up projects is only half the work. Review this report monthly to catch underperforming jobs before they get worse.

## Practice Exercise

Imagine you are a landscaping contractor. A customer named "Green Valley HOA" has hired you for a seasonal cleanup project.

1. Enable Projects in Account and Settings > Advanced.
2. Create a project called "Spring Cleanup 2025" linked to Green Valley HOA.
3. Create an invoice for $3,200 and assign it to the project.
4. Enter an expense of $450 for mulch (vendor: "Local Nursery") and link it to the project. Mark it as billable.
5. Add a time entry of 8 hours at $75/hour for "Landscaping Services" linked to the project.
6. Open the project and review the Income, Expenses, and Profit summary.

## Key Takeaways

- Projects group all invoices, expenses, bills, and time entries for a specific job into one profitability view.
- Projects must be enabled in Account and Settings > Advanced and require a Plus or Advanced subscription.
- Every project is linked to a customer — you can have multiple projects per customer.
- Marking expenses and time as Billable ensures they flow through to the client's invoice automatically.
- The Project Profitability report shows your true margin per job and is essential for service businesses and contractors who need to know if their pricing is working.
