# Time Tracking and Billable Hours

QuickBooks Online's time tracking tools let you record exactly how many hours your team works, assign that time to customers or projects, and convert billable hours directly into invoices — so no revenue slips through the cracks.

## What You'll Learn
- How to enable time tracking in QuickBooks Online settings
- How to enter a single time activity manually
- How to use weekly timesheets for faster bulk entry
- How to assign time to specific customers and projects
- How to mark time as billable and add it to a client invoice
- How to run the Time Activities by Employee report
- How time tracking connects to payroll

---

## What Is Time Tracking in QuickBooks Online?

Time tracking lets you record hours worked by employees, contractors, or yourself — and attach those hours to a customer, project, or service type. Once recorded, time entries become the source of truth for two things:

1. **Billing clients** — billable hours flow directly into invoices so you charge for every hour worked.
2. **Paying employees** — time data can feed into payroll so wages are calculated from actual hours logged.

Even if you do not bill by the hour, time tracking helps you understand your true labor cost per project — a key input for profitability analysis alongside the Projects feature covered in Lesson 7.2.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Time tracking in QuickBooks Online is best suited for businesses with a relatively small team doing straightforward time entry. If you have a larger workforce, complex scheduling needs, or require GPS tracking and mobile clock-in, consider a dedicated time tracking app — such as TSheets (now QuickBooks Time), Clockify, or Harvest — that integrates directly with QBO.

## Enabling Time Tracking

Time tracking is off by default. Here is how to turn it on:

1. Click the **Gear icon** (Settings) in the upper-right corner of QuickBooks Online.
2. Under "Your Company," click **Account and Settings**.
3. Click the **Advanced** tab.
4. Scroll to the **Time tracking** section.
5. Click the pencil (Edit) icon.
6. Toggle **Add Service field to timesheets** to ON — this lets you specify what type of work was performed on each time entry.
7. Toggle **Make Single-Time Activity Billable to Customer** to ON — this ensures time entries can flow to invoices.
8. In the **First day of work week** field, set your preferred week start day (typically Sunday or Monday).
9. Click **Save**, then click **Done**.

## Adding a Single Time Activity

A Single Time Activity is a one-off time entry — ideal for logging time on a specific task as you complete it.

**To enter a single time activity:**

1. Click **+ New** in the upper-left corner.
2. Under the "Employees" column, click **Single Time Activity**.
3. In the **Name** field, select the employee or contractor who performed the work.
4. Enter the **Date** the work was performed.
5. In the **Customer/Project** field, select the customer or project this time relates to.
6. In the **Service** field, select the type of work performed (e.g., "Consulting," "Installation," "Design Work"). This determines how the time appears on the invoice.
7. In the **Time** field, enter the hours and minutes worked. You can either type the duration or use the built-in timer by clicking the clock icon.
8. Check the **Billable** checkbox if this time will be charged to the client. Leave it unchecked for internal or non-billable work.
9. If billing at a specific hourly rate, verify or enter the **Hourly Rate** field that appears when Billable is checked.
10. Add any relevant notes in the **Description** field.
11. Click **Save and Close**.

## Using Weekly Timesheets

If your team tracks time across multiple customers or projects each week, weekly timesheets are faster than entering individual activities one at a time.

**To use a weekly timesheet:**

1. Click **+ New** > **Weekly Timesheet** (under the Employees column).
2. Select the **Employee or Vendor** from the Name dropdown.
3. Set the **Week** using the date navigation at the top.
4. Each row on the timesheet represents one Customer/Project + Service combination. Click **Add lines** to add additional rows.
5. For each row, select:
   - Customer/Project
   - Service item
   - Whether the time is Billable (checkbox on the left)
6. Enter hours for each day of the week across the columns (Mon, Tue, Wed, etc.).
7. The timesheet automatically totals hours per row and per day.
8. Click **Save and Close** when the week is complete.

Weekly timesheets create the same individual time activity records as single entries — they are just a more efficient way to enter a week's worth of time at once.

## Adding Billable Time to an Invoice

Once billable time has been recorded, you can pull it directly into a client invoice without retyping anything.

1. Click **+ New > Invoice**.
2. In the **Customer** field, select the client whose billable time you want to invoice.
3. A drawer or banner will appear on the right side (or at the top) of the invoice saying "X billable activities available." Click **Add** to open it.
4. A list of all unbilled time entries for that customer will appear. Review each entry.
5. Check the box next to each time entry you want to include on this invoice.
6. Click **Add Selected** (or **Add All**).
7. The time entries populate as line items on the invoice, with the service type as the description and the hours × rate as the amount.
8. Review the invoice, adjust descriptions if needed, and click **Save and Send**.

Once added to an invoice, the time entries are marked as billed and will not appear again in future invoice prompts for that customer.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** If a client asks for a detailed breakdown of hours, use the Description field on each time entry when you record it — not just the Service type. Descriptions like "Strategy call with marketing team re: Q3 campaign (2hrs)" give clients the transparency they need and reduce billing disputes.

## Running the Time Activities by Employee Report

Once you have time entries in QuickBooks, you can run reports to analyze how hours are being spent.

**To run the Time Activities by Employee report:**

1. Click **Reports** in the left navigation menu.
2. In the search bar, type **"Time Activities by Employee"** and click the report.
3. Set the **date range** for the period you want to review.
4. Click **Run Report**.

The report shows:
- Each employee or contractor's name
- Time entries grouped by date
- Customer/Project assigned to each entry
- Hours worked per entry
- Whether the time was billable or non-billable
- The hourly rate and total billable amount

You can also run **Time Activities by Customer** to see all hours logged against a specific client — useful for reviewing before generating an invoice or checking whether you are staying within a project's scope.

## Connecting Time Tracking to Payroll

If you run payroll through QuickBooks Online Payroll, tracked hours can feed directly into payroll calculations:

- Hourly employees: Time entries for the pay period can be imported into payroll runs so wages are calculated from logged hours rather than manually entered.
- When running payroll, QuickBooks will prompt you to import timesheet hours for hourly employees.
- Salaried employees: Time tracking still works for project costing purposes, but it does not affect their paycheck calculation.

For businesses using a third-party payroll provider (e.g., Gusto, ADP), time data in QBO does not automatically export — you will need to export a time report and manually enter hours into your payroll system, or use an integration if one is available.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Forgetting to check the Billable checkbox.** If you track time but forget to mark it as billable, it will never appear on a client invoice. Build a habit of checking this for every client-facing entry.
- **Not assigning time to a customer or project.** Time entries without a customer assignment are essentially invisible for billing and project reporting. Always link time to a customer or project.
- **Mixing billable and internal time on the same timesheet row.** Create separate rows for billable client work and internal (non-billable) tasks like admin or training. This keeps your billable hours report clean.
- **Waiting too long to invoice billable time.** The longer you wait, the more likely a client is to question an old charge. Set a regular cadence — weekly or monthly — for reviewing unbilled time and generating invoices.
- **Letting time entries pile up without reviewing.** Run the Time Activities by Employee report at least monthly to verify entries are accurate and properly assigned before they become difficult to trace.

## Practice Exercise

Walk through the complete time tracking and billing workflow:

1. Enable time tracking in Account and Settings > Advanced.
2. Create a Single Time Activity: Employee = yourself, Customer = "Acme Corp," Service = "Consulting," 3.5 hours, Billable at $125/hour, Date = today.
3. Create a Weekly Timesheet for yourself covering the current week. Add two rows: 6 hours for "Acme Corp / Consulting" and 2 hours for "Internal / Admin" (not billable).
4. Create a new Invoice for Acme Corp. When prompted, add all available billable time entries to the invoice.
5. Review the invoice to confirm the line items and amounts are correct, then save it.
6. Run the Time Activities by Employee report for the current month and verify all entries appear.

## Key Takeaways

- Time tracking must be enabled in Account and Settings > Advanced before it is available on transaction forms.
- Single Time Activities work for one-off entries; Weekly Timesheets are faster for logging an entire week across multiple customers and services.
- Always assign time to a Customer or Project and check the Billable box for any work you plan to invoice.
- Billable time flows into invoices automatically — when you create an invoice for a customer, QBO will prompt you to add any unbilled time entries in one click.
- The Time Activities by Employee and Time Activities by Customer reports give you visibility into how hours are being spent and help ensure nothing billable is overlooked.
