# Sales and Expense Reports

Sales and expense reports give you a detailed breakdown of where your revenue comes from and where your money goes, helping you identify your best customers, your top-selling products, and your biggest cost drivers.

## What You'll Learn

- How to run Sales by Customer Summary and Sales by Product/Service Summary
- How to run the Expenses by Vendor Summary
- How to filter and customize each report for the dates and categories you need
- How to compare periods to spot growth and decline
- How to identify your top customers and most significant expense categories
- How to spot trends that affect profitability
- How to export reports to Excel for deeper analysis

---

## Why Sales and Expense Reports Matter

The Profit & Loss gives you the big picture — total revenue minus total expenses equals net income. But it doesn't answer questions like:

- Which customer accounts for 40% of my revenue — and what happens if they leave?
- Which product line has the highest sales volume?
- Am I spending too much with one vendor?
- Are my software subscription costs creeping up unnoticed?

Sales and expense reports answer these questions with specificity. They move you from knowing *that* something changed to knowing *why* — and with that knowledge, you can make real business decisions.

## Sales by Customer Summary

This report shows your total revenue from each customer over a selected time period — invaluable for understanding customer concentration and identifying your most valuable relationships.

### Running the Report

1. Click **Reports** in the left-hand navigation menu.
2. Search for **Sales by Customer Summary** and select it.
3. Set the **Report period** — This Month, This Quarter, This Year, or a custom date range.
4. Click **Run report**.
5. The report defaults to showing all customers alphabetically. To sort by revenue, click the **Total** column header to sort highest to lowest.
6. To filter to a specific customer group or location, click **Customize** at the top right, then use the **Filter** section to narrow results.
7. Click any customer name to drill into a detail view showing every transaction with that customer in the period.
8. To compare periods, click **Customize**, go to **Columns**, and add a **Previous Period** or **Previous Year** comparison column.

### What to Look For

- **Customer concentration risk:** If one customer represents more than 20-25% of total revenue, your business is dangerously dependent on that relationship. Diversification should be a strategic goal.
- **Declining customers:** Compare this year to last. Customers whose spending has dropped significantly may be at risk of leaving — reach out proactively.
- **New vs. returning revenue:** Growth is healthiest when it comes from both new customers and increased spending by existing ones.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Sort the Sales by Customer report from highest to lowest and look at your top 10 customers. These relationships deserve VIP treatment — personal check-ins, early access to new services, and priority response times. Losing one top customer can wipe out months of new business development.

## Sales by Product/Service Summary

This report shows your revenue broken down by the items and services you sell — essential for understanding which offerings drive your business.

### Running the Report

1. Click **Reports** in the left-hand navigation menu.
2. Search for **Sales by Product/Service Summary** and select it.
3. Set the **Report period** and click **Run report**.
4. Review the columns: Product/Service name, Quantity sold, Amount, and percentage of total.
5. Click **Customize** to add a comparison period column (last year, last quarter).
6. Click any product or service name to see the detail — which customers bought it, on which dates, at what price.
7. To filter to a specific product category, click **Customize > Filter > Product/Service** and select your categories.

[IMAGE: QuickBooks Sales by Product/Service Summary report with quantity and revenue columns, showing percentage of total for each item]

### What to Look For

- **Your revenue drivers:** Which products or services generate the most revenue? These deserve investment, promotion, and inventory priority.
- **Low performers:** Items with very few sales or low revenue may be cluttering your offering and confusing customers. Consider consolidating or sunsetting them.
- **Margin by product:** Revenue volume alone doesn't tell the full story. A high-volume product with thin margins may be less valuable than a lower-volume premium service. Cross-reference with your COGS data.
- **Price consistency:** If the same service is billed at different rates to different customers, this report makes that visible — useful for enforcing pricing discipline.

## Expenses by Vendor Summary

This report shows how much you've spent with each vendor over a period — your primary tool for understanding where your money goes and whether vendor relationships are appropriately sized.

### Running the Report

1. Click **Reports** in the left-hand navigation menu.
2. Search for **Expenses by Vendor Summary** and select it.
3. Set the **Report period** and click **Run report**.
4. Sort by **Total** (click the column header) to see your largest vendors at the top.
5. Click any vendor name to see every transaction with that vendor in the period — dates, amounts, expense categories, and bill or payment numbers.
6. Click **Customize** to filter by expense account, location, or class if you use those features.
7. Add a comparison period column to see how vendor spending has changed year over year.

### What to Look For

- **Vendor concentration:** Are you heavily dependent on one supplier? Supply chain risk is real — having a backup vendor for critical inputs is worth the relationship investment.
- **Unexpected growth in vendor spending:** A vendor whose total jumps 50% year over year may reflect legitimate business growth — or it may mean purchases weren't reviewed carefully.
- **Subscriptions and recurring charges:** Small recurring charges add up. Filter for software vendors and subscription services to see the total annual cost of your tech stack.
- **Vendors you no longer use:** If a vendor has charges but you don't recognize the relationship, investigate. Auto-renewing subscriptions for cancelled services are a common culprit.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Run the Expenses by Vendor Summary at the end of each year and schedule a vendor audit. For every vendor spending over $1,000 annually, ask: Is this still delivering value? Are we getting a competitive price? Could we consolidate this vendor relationship with another we're already using?

## Comparing Periods to Spot Trends

All three of these reports become significantly more powerful when you compare periods. Here's how to build a comparison into any of them:

1. Open any of the three reports and click **Customize** (top right).
2. Scroll to the **Columns** section.
3. Check **Previous period** to compare to the period immediately before (e.g., if you're looking at Q2, this adds Q1).
4. Or check **Previous year** to compare to the same period one year ago.
5. QuickBooks will add columns showing the dollar change and percentage change automatically.
6. Click **Run report** to apply.

Use period comparisons to answer: Is this customer spending more or less than last year? Is this expense category growing faster than revenue? Is a product losing sales volume?

## Exporting to Excel for Further Analysis

QuickBooks reports give you a solid starting view, but Excel gives you the flexibility to slice the data further — pivot tables, custom charts, combined datasets.

1. Run any sales or expense report with your desired date range and filters.
2. Click the **Export** icon (top right of the report — it looks like a grid with an arrow).
3. Select **Export to Excel**.
4. QuickBooks downloads an `.xlsx` file to your computer.
5. Open the file in Excel. The data comes in with rows and columns exactly matching the on-screen report.
6. From here, you can create pivot tables, add charts, combine data from multiple reports, or build a custom dashboard.

**Useful Excel analyses to build from these exports:**
- Customer revenue ranked by amount with % of total
- Year-over-year expense growth by category
- Revenue by product line as a pie or bar chart for presentations
- Month-by-month sales trend line for a specific customer

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Only looking at totals without drilling into detail.** The summary is useful for the overview, but the detail is where you find the stories — and the problems.
- **Not running these reports regularly.** Quarterly is a minimum; monthly is better. Trends are much easier to spot and address early.
- **Forgetting to filter by date range.** Always confirm the period shown at the top of the report matches what you intend to analyze.
- **Ignoring customer concentration risk.** A single customer making up 30-40% of revenue is a major business vulnerability that the Sales by Customer report makes impossible to ignore — if you actually run it.
- **Exporting to Excel and never using it.** Exporting the data is the first step. Build at least one meaningful chart or analysis from the export, or the data will just sit in your downloads folder.

## Key Takeaways

- Sales by Customer Summary reveals your most valuable customer relationships and any dangerous revenue concentration.
- Sales by Product/Service Summary shows which offerings drive your business and which may be underperforming.
- Expenses by Vendor Summary highlights your largest spending relationships and surfaces unexpected cost increases.
- Period comparison columns (prior year, prior period) turn static reports into trend analysis tools.
- Exporting to Excel unlocks additional analysis capabilities including pivot tables, charts, and multi-report data combinations.
