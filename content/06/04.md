# AR and AP Aging Reports

The AR and AP Aging reports tell you exactly who owes you money and who you owe money to — broken down by how long those balances have been outstanding — making them your most powerful tools for managing cash flow week to week.

- How to prioritize collections using the AR aging report
- How to prioritize payments using the AP aging report

## Understanding Aging Reports

An aging report organizes outstanding balances into time "buckets" based on how long each invoice or bill has been unpaid. The standard buckets are:

- **Current:** Not yet due
- **1-30 days past due**
- **31-60 days past due**
- **61-90 days past due**
- **91 and over days past due**

The further right the money sits, the harder it typically is to collect (for AR) — and the more strained your vendor relationships become (for AP). Reviewing these reports weekly is one of the highest-value habits a small business owner can develop.

## Running the AR Aging Reports

### AR Aging Summary

The summary shows each customer and their total balance distributed across aging buckets. This is your quick overview.

1. Click **Reports** in the left-hand navigation menu.
2. Search for **Accounts Receivable Aging Summary** and select it.
3. Set the **Report date** — use today's date or end of last month for consistency.
4. Set the **Aging method**: by **Due Date** (most common — shows how overdue the invoice is) or by **Invoice Date**.
5. Set the **Days per aging period** — the default is 30, which creates the standard 1-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+ buckets.
6. Click **Run report**.
7. Review the columns from left to right. The rightmost columns (61-90 and 90+) need the most urgent attention.

### AR Aging Detail

The detail report shows every individual open invoice rather than customer totals.

1. Search for **Accounts Receivable Aging Detail** and select it.
2. Set the same date and aging settings as the summary.
3. Click **Run report**.
4. Use the detail report when you need invoice-level information for collection calls — you can see exact invoice numbers, amounts, and dates.
5. Click any invoice to open it and review the details or send a reminder directly from the record.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Run the AR Aging Summary every Monday morning. Spending 15 minutes reviewing who owes you money at the start of each week — and following up with anyone 30+ days overdue — will do more to improve your cash flow than almost anything else.

## Running the AP Aging Reports

### AP Aging Summary

The AP Aging Summary shows each vendor and how much you owe them, organized by aging bucket.

1. Click **Reports** in the left-hand navigation.
2. Search for **Accounts Payable Aging Summary** and select it.
3. Set the **Report date** to today or end of period.
4. Set aging method to **Due Date**.
5. Click **Run report**.
6. Focus on the **Current** column first — these are bills due soon that you need to plan cash for. Then review the 1-30 column for anything already past due.

### AP Aging Detail

1. Search for **Accounts Payable Aging Detail** and select it.
2. Set the same report date and aging method.
3. Click **Run report**.
4. This report shows each individual unpaid bill — vendor, bill number, date, due date, and aging bucket.
5. Use this when you need to decide which specific bills to pay this week given available cash.

## Reading the Aging Buckets

**Current:** These are invoices or bills not yet due. For AR, you're waiting for the payment window. For AP, you have time — plan these into your cash schedule.

**1-30 days past due (AR):** Send a friendly reminder. Many customers simply forgot. A quick email referencing the invoice number and amount often produces same-day payment.

**31-60 days past due (AR):** Make a phone call. At this point, a passive email isn't enough. Personal outreach gets results.

**61-90 days past due (AR):** Escalate. Consider putting the customer on credit hold — stop delivering new work until the outstanding balance is addressed. Send a formal collections notice.

**91+ days past due (AR):** At this stage, collectability is significantly lower. Consider involving a collections agency or writing off the balance. Continuing to extend credit to chronic late-payers is not a business strategy — it's financing them for free.

**1-30 days past due (AP):** Pay promptly to protect vendor relationships. Late payments can result in late fees, lost discounts, and vendors who deprioritize your orders.

**31+ days past due (AP):** Communicate proactively with vendors if you can't pay. Most vendors would rather set up a payment plan than lose a customer — but only if you reach out before they have to chase you.

## Prioritizing Collections with AR Aging

Not all overdue invoices deserve equal urgency. Prioritize your collection efforts using these criteria:

1. **Amount owed:** Larger balances get attention first. A $10,000 overdue invoice matters more than a $200 one.
2. **Days outstanding:** Older balances are harder to collect — address them before they age further.
3. **Customer history:** A reliable long-term customer who is 35 days past due on one invoice may just need a reminder. A new customer with multiple overdue invoices is a pattern.
4. **Future relationship:** Weigh the collection action against the ongoing value of the customer relationship.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Add a custom message to your invoice template reminding customers of your payment terms and providing an easy online payment link. Friction-free payment options (credit card, ACH) consistently reduce average days to pay.

## Using AR and AP Together for Cash Flow Management

The real power of these two reports is using them side by side:

- **Total AR (money coming in)** vs. **Total AP (money going out)** gives you a rough cash flow forecast for the near term.
- If your AR aging is mostly current and your AP aging has items in the 31-60 bucket, you likely have a collection lag — you need to accelerate customer payments before vendor bills pile up.
- If your AP is mostly current but AR has large 61-90 buckets, you're at risk of using cash or credit to pay bills while customers haven't paid yet.

Review both reports together weekly as a package to make informed decisions about timing payments and pressing for collections.

## Sharing Aging Reports with Owners and Managers

Many business owners want a weekly summary of who owes money and who needs to be paid. The best format is a simple export:

1. Run the AR Aging Summary.
2. Click **Export** (top right of the report) and choose **Export to Excel** or **Export to PDF**.
3. Repeat for the AP Aging Summary.
4. Email both to the owner or operations manager with a brief note highlighting any balances over 60 days.

Alternatively, you can email reports directly from QuickBooks — see Lesson 6.8 for details on sending reports by email.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Running aging reports as of a past date but not noting the discrepancy.** Always label aging reports clearly with the "as of" date — a report from last Friday is already stale.
- **Ignoring the 91+ bucket.** Old receivables don't collect themselves. If you don't have a process for aging accounts, those balances will sit until they're worthless.
- **Not separating retainers from invoice balances.** Customer deposits or retainers held on account can skew AR aging. Make sure unapplied credits are visible and accounted for.
- **Paying AP without checking cash first.** The AP aging detail shows you what's owed, but always confirm your cash position before scheduling payments.
- **Using summary instead of detail for collection calls.** The summary gives totals by customer; the detail gives you the specific invoice number and amount you need for an effective collection conversation.

## Key Takeaways

- The AR Aging report shows money customers owe you, sorted by how overdue each balance is — your primary tool for managing collections.
- The AP Aging report shows money you owe vendors, sorted by due date — your primary tool for managing payment timing.
- Standard aging buckets are Current, 1-30, 31-60, 61-90, and 91+ days past due.
- Use the Summary for weekly review and trend spotting; use the Detail for specific collection calls and payment decisions.
- Reviewing both reports together weekly gives you a real-time view of near-term cash flow and helps you decide when to push for collections and when to make vendor payments.
