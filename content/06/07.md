# Customizing and Saving Reports

QuickBooks comes with dozens of standard reports, but the real power comes from tailoring those reports to show exactly the data you need — and then saving your customizations so you never have to rebuild them from scratch.

## What You'll Learn

- How to customize report date ranges and comparison periods
- How to filter reports by customer, vendor, class, or location
- How to change display columns and add or remove rows
- How to use grouping options to reorganize report data
- How to save a custom report and access it later
- How to find your saved reports in the Custom Reports tab
- How to schedule automatic report delivery via email

---

## Why Customize Reports?

The standard QuickBooks reports are built for the average business — which means they're perfect for no business in particular. Customization lets you:

- Show only the accounts or customers that matter to your review
- Compare the periods that are meaningful for your business cycle
- Group data by department, location, or project
- Remove clutter (accounts with zero balances, inactive customers)
- Create a repeatable weekly or monthly report package that takes one click to run

Every minute you spend setting up a custom report correctly saves you five minutes of reformatting every time you need it going forward.

## Customizing Date Ranges and Comparison Periods

Every report starts with a date range. Changing it is the most basic customization.

1. Open any report from the **Reports** menu.
2. At the top of the report, find the **Report period** dropdown. Click it to choose from presets: This Month, Last Month, This Quarter, Last Quarter, This Year, Last Year, Last 365 Days, or a custom range.
3. For a custom range, click the start and end date fields directly and type or select dates from the calendar picker.
4. To add a comparison column, click **Compare another period** and choose: **Previous period**, **Previous year**, **Year-to-date**, or **Custom** (enter specific start/end dates).
5. Click **Run report** to apply.

For deeper customization, click the **Customize** button (top right of any report). The Customize panel opens on the right side of the screen — this is where all the powerful options live.

## Filtering Reports

Filters let you narrow a report to show only the data relevant to your question. This is particularly useful for large businesses with many customers, vendors, or accounts.

### Filtering by Customer or Vendor

1. Open a report and click **Customize**.
2. In the Customize panel, scroll to the **Filter** section.
3. Click the dropdown for the filter type you want — **Customer**, **Vendor**, **Employee**, **Account**, **Product/Service**, etc.
4. Select one or more specific values from the list, or type to search.
5. Click **Run report** to apply the filter.

**Example:** Run a P&L filtered to only transactions associated with one specific customer — useful for job costing or profitability analysis by client.

### Filtering by Class or Location

If your business uses **Classes** (departments, project types, funding sources) or **Locations** (multiple offices, stores, or regions), you can filter any report to show only one class or location.

1. Click **Customize > Filter**.
2. Choose **Class** or **Location** from the filter dropdown.
3. Select the class or location you want to isolate.
4. Click **Run report**.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Classes and Locations are optional features in QuickBooks, but they are game-changers for businesses with multiple revenue streams or physical locations. If you're not using them yet and your business has distinct departments or service lines, talk to your accountant about setting them up. They make every report significantly more useful.

## Changing Display Columns

For reports that display data in columns (like the P&L with months across the top, or the AR Aging with aging buckets), you can control which columns appear.

1. Open a report and click **Customize**.
2. In the Customize panel, find the **Columns** section (the location varies by report type).
3. For a P&L or Balance Sheet: choose to display by **Total only**, **Month**, **Quarter**, or **Year**.
4. For comparison reports: check boxes for **Previous period**, **Previous year**, **% of total**, or **% change**.
5. For transaction-level reports: use the column checkboxes to add or remove fields like Memo, Account, Class, or Location.
6. Click **Run report** to apply.

**The "% of income" column** is especially useful on the P&L — it shows every line item as a percentage of total revenue, making it easy to see expense ratios at a glance and compare them to industry benchmarks or prior periods.

## Adding, Removing, and Grouping Rows

### Showing or Hiding Zero-Balance Accounts

By default, some reports show every account in your chart of accounts, even ones with zero activity. To clean up the view:

1. Click **Customize**.
2. Find the **Rows/Columns** section.
3. Set **Show rows** to **Non-zero** or **Active** to hide accounts with no activity in the period.
4. Click **Run report**.

### Grouping Options

Grouping changes how rows are organized in the report — useful for reorganizing data by customer, vendor, class, or time period.

1. Click **Customize**.
2. Find the **Group by** option (available in transaction reports and some summary reports).
3. Choose your grouping: by Customer, Vendor, Class, Location, Month, Week, etc.
4. Click **Run report**.

**Example:** Run an expense report grouped by Class to see total spending by department instead of by vendor.

## Saving a Custom Report

Once you've built a customized report you'll use regularly, save it so you can access it with one click next time.

1. After customizing and running the report, click **Save customization** (top right of the report screen).
2. A dialog box appears. Enter a descriptive name — for example, "Monthly P&L with YOY Comparison" or "AR Aging — Weekly Review."
3. Optionally, choose a **Custom report group** to organize your saved reports (e.g., "Management Reports," "Weekly Reviews," "Tax Prep").
4. If you want to share this report with other users in your QuickBooks company, check **Share with all users**.
5. Click **Save**.

The report is now saved and can be run again anytime without re-entering all the settings.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Create a saved report group called "Monthly Close" and save your standard month-end reports there — P&L with YOY comparison, Balance Sheet, AR Aging Summary, AP Aging Summary, and Budget vs. Actuals. Running your full monthly close becomes a five-click process instead of a 20-minute setup session.

## Accessing the Custom Reports Tab

All your saved custom reports live in one place:

1. Click **Reports** in the left-hand navigation.
2. At the top of the Reports page, click the **Custom reports** tab (next to Standard and Management Reports).
3. Your saved reports are listed here, organized by the groups you created.
4. Click any report name to run it immediately with your saved settings.
5. Click the dropdown arrow next to any report to **Edit**, **Delete**, or **Share** it.

## Scheduling Automatic Report Delivery via Email

QuickBooks Online can automatically run and email reports on a schedule — daily, weekly, or monthly — so the right people always have current information without having to log in and run reports manually.

1. Open the **Custom Reports** tab under Reports.
2. Find the saved custom report you want to schedule.
3. Click the dropdown arrow next to the report name and select **Edit**.
4. In the Edit dialog, find the **Set email schedule** section and toggle it on.
5. Set the **Frequency**: Daily, Weekly, or Monthly.
6. Choose the **Day** (e.g., every Monday, the first of each month).
7. Set the **Time** for delivery.
8. Enter the **Email addresses** to send the report to — you can add multiple recipients.
9. Write a custom **Subject** line and optional **Message** for the email.
10. Click **Save**.

QuickBooks will now automatically run and email this report on the schedule you set. Recipients receive a PDF attachment of the report in their inbox.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Customizing a report without saving it.** All your filter and column settings will be lost when you navigate away. Always click Save Customization before leaving the report.
- **Giving saved reports vague names.** A report called "Custom Report 1" tells you nothing. Use names like "P&L — Monthly with Prior Year Comparison" so you know exactly what you're opening.
- **Over-filtering to the point of missing context.** Filtering to one customer or one account is powerful for specific questions, but make sure you also maintain a standard unfiltered view for the full picture.
- **Scheduling reports to the wrong email addresses.** When setting up scheduled delivery, double-check recipient email addresses — it's easy to mistype, and sensitive financial data going to the wrong inbox is a real problem.
- **Not reviewing saved reports periodically.** Business needs change. Review your saved custom reports quarterly and delete or update any that are no longer relevant.

## Key Takeaways

- The Customize panel in any QuickBooks report gives you control over date ranges, comparison periods, filters, columns, rows, and grouping.
- Filters by customer, vendor, class, or location let you narrow reports to exactly the data you need for a specific question.
- Save customized reports using the **Save Customization** button so you can re-run them instantly without rebuilding settings each time.
- All saved reports are accessible under the **Custom Reports** tab in the Reports section, organized by groups you define.
- Scheduled email delivery lets QuickBooks automatically send reports to owners, managers, or accountants on a recurring schedule without any manual effort.
