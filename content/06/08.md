# Exporting and Sharing Reports

QuickBooks makes it easy to get your financial reports out of the system and into the hands of the people who need them — whether that's a PDF for your banker, an Excel file for deeper analysis, or a polished management report package for your board.

## What You'll Learn

- How to export reports to PDF for sharing and archiving
- How to export reports to Excel or CSV for further analysis
- How to print reports directly from QuickBooks
- How to email reports directly from QuickBooks Online
- What the Management Reports feature is and how to use it
- How to create a professional management report package
- How to share reports securely with your accountant
- How to set up recurring report emails for consistent delivery

---

## Why Report Sharing Matters

Generating a report is only half the job. The real value comes from getting that information to the right person at the right time in the right format. A business owner reviewing the P&L on their phone needs a clean PDF. An analyst building projections needs an Excel export. A banker reviewing your loan application needs a formal management report package. QuickBooks supports all of these scenarios — you just need to know where to look.

## Exporting to PDF

PDF export is the standard format for sharing financial reports with anyone outside your QuickBooks system — lenders, investors, attorneys, or business partners who just need to read the numbers.

1. Open any report in QuickBooks Online (run it with the date range and filters you want first).
2. Click the **Export** icon at the top right of the report. It looks like a small grid with an upward arrow, or may be labeled **Export**.
3. Select **Export to PDF** from the dropdown menu.
4. QuickBooks generates the PDF and downloads it to your computer automatically.
5. Open the downloaded file to verify the formatting looks correct before sending.
6. Send via email as an attachment, upload to a shared folder, or save to your document management system.

**PDF export tips:**
- The PDF will match exactly what you see on screen — so collapse or expand sections before exporting to get the level of detail you want.
- For Balance Sheets and P&Ls, use **Collapse** to show summary-level data for external audiences, and **Expand** for detailed internal review.
- Name your PDF files clearly before saving: "ABC Company P&L Jan-Dec 2025.pdf" is far more useful than "QuickBooks Report.pdf."

## Exporting to Excel or CSV

Excel export gives you the raw data in a spreadsheet, ready for pivot tables, charts, formulas, and deeper analysis beyond what QuickBooks offers natively.

1. Open and run the report you want to export.
2. Click the **Export** icon (top right of the report).
3. Select **Export to Excel**. QuickBooks downloads an `.xlsx` file.
4. Open the file in Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets.
5. The data comes in pre-formatted with the same rows and columns as the on-screen report.

**What to do with the Excel export:**
- Build charts showing revenue or expense trends over time
- Create pivot tables to slice data by customer, product, or period
- Combine data from multiple reports (e.g., merge a P&L export and a Sales by Customer export into one workbook)
- Format a custom management report template with your company branding
- Build a simple cash flow forecast using the AR and AP aging data

**CSV format:** If you need a plain comma-separated values file (for importing into another system, a database, or a BI tool), some QuickBooks reports offer a CSV option alongside Excel. Look for it in the same Export dropdown.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** When exporting to Excel for recurring monthly reviews, create a master workbook with your standard template and simply paste in the new monthly data each period. This keeps your charts and formulas intact and lets you build multi-month trend views over time without starting from scratch each month.

## Printing Reports

For situations where a paper copy is needed — client meetings, filing systems, or presentations without a screen — QuickBooks makes printing straightforward.

1. Open and run the report with the settings you want.
2. Click the **Print** icon (top right of the report, next to the Export icon) — or use **Ctrl+P** (Windows) / **Cmd+P** (Mac).
3. Your browser's print dialog opens. Choose your printer, paper size (Letter is standard for most financial reports), and orientation (Landscape often works better for wide reports like the Balance Sheet with comparison columns).
4. Click **Print**.

**Printing tips:**
- Use **Fit to page** or adjust the scale percentage in the print dialog to prevent reports from being cut off at the right margin.
- For multi-page reports, preview all pages before printing to ensure nothing is missing.
- Consider printing to PDF (using your system's "Print to PDF" option) as an alternative to physical paper — it preserves the layout without using paper.

## Emailing Reports Directly from QuickBooks

QuickBooks Online lets you email any report directly from within the platform, without downloading and re-attaching files.

1. Open and run the report you want to send.
2. Click the **Email** icon at the top right of the report (it looks like an envelope), or find the email option in the same toolbar as Export and Print.
3. A compose window opens within QuickBooks.
4. Enter the **recipient's email address** in the To field.
5. Edit the **subject line** if needed — QuickBooks pre-fills it with the report name and date.
6. Add a message in the body if desired.
7. Choose the format: **PDF** is the default and most common choice.
8. Click **Send**.

The recipient receives an email with the report attached as a PDF. They do not need a QuickBooks account to view it.

## The Management Reports Feature

Management Reports is a premium feature in QuickBooks Online that lets you create polished, branded report packages — the kind you'd present to a board, a bank, or a potential investor. It goes far beyond a simple PDF export.

**What Management Reports includes:**
- A cover page with your company name and logo
- A table of contents
- Custom text pages (for executive summaries, commentary, or notes)
- Multiple financial reports combined into one document (P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow, etc.)
- Professional formatting with consistent headers and footers

To access Management Reports:

1. Click **Reports** in the left-hand navigation.
2. Click the **Management Reports** tab at the top of the Reports page (next to Standard and Custom Reports).
3. QuickBooks provides pre-built packages: **Company Overview** (P&L + Balance Sheet) and **Sales Performance** (sales reports). You can use these as-is or create your own.
4. Click any existing package to preview it, or click **New** to create a custom package from scratch.

---

## Creating a Management Report Package

1. From the Management Reports tab, click **New** (top right).
2. Give the report package a name — for example, "Monthly Board Package" or "Quarterly Lender Report."
3. Set the **Report period** that will apply to all reports in the package.
4. Click **Add a report** to add individual reports to the package — choose from P&L, Balance Sheet, Statement of Cash Flows, and others.
5. Use the drag handle to reorder reports within the package.
6. Click **Add text** to insert custom pages — write an executive summary, add commentary on key variances, or include management discussion sections.
7. Click **Edit cover** to add your company logo, adjust colors, and customize the cover page.
8. When your package is complete, click **View** to preview the final document.
9. Click **Export as PDF** to download the full package as a single polished PDF.
10. Click **Send** to email the package directly from QuickBooks.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Create a standard monthly Management Report package that includes your P&L, Balance Sheet, and a two-paragraph executive summary you update each month. Sending this to your business owner or board on a fixed schedule — say, the 10th of each month for the prior month — builds financial transparency and demonstrates professional financial management.

## Sharing Reports with Your Accountant

QuickBooks Online makes it easy to give your accountant direct access to your books — which is usually better than emailing reports back and forth.

**Option 1: Invite your accountant as a user**
1. Go to **Settings (Gear icon) > Manage Users**.
2. Click **Accounting Firms** tab.
3. Click **Invite** and enter your accountant's email address.
4. Your accountant receives an invitation to connect their QuickBooks Accountant account to your company file. They get read/write access without needing your login credentials.

**Option 2: Export and share specific reports**
If you only need to share a specific report (not grant ongoing access):
1. Run the report, export as PDF or Excel.
2. Share via email, Dropbox, Google Drive, or your preferred file sharing platform.
3. Label the file clearly with company name, report type, and date range.

**What accountants typically need at year-end:**
- P&L for the full fiscal year
- Balance Sheet as of December 31 (or your fiscal year end)
- General Ledger detail for the full year
- Bank and credit card reconciliation reports

## Setting Up Recurring Report Emails

For reports that need to go out on a schedule — weekly AR aging to the collections team, monthly P&L to the owner, quarterly financials to investors — QuickBooks can automate the delivery.

1. First, save the report as a custom report (see Lesson 6.7 for detailed steps).
2. Go to the **Custom Reports** tab under Reports.
3. Click the dropdown arrow next to the saved report and select **Edit**.
4. Toggle on **Set email schedule**.
5. Choose frequency: **Daily**, **Weekly**, or **Monthly**.
6. Set the day and time.
7. Enter recipient email addresses (comma-separated for multiple recipients).
8. Write a subject line and optional message.
9. Click **Save**.

QuickBooks runs the report automatically and emails it as a PDF on the schedule you set. No manual action required after the initial setup.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Exporting reports without verifying the date range first.** Always confirm the report period shown at the top of the report before exporting — it is easy to accidentally export a report covering the wrong time period.
- **Sending unformatted Excel exports to non-accountants.** Raw exported data is confusing for people who aren't comfortable with spreadsheets. Use PDF for external sharing unless the recipient specifically requested Excel.
- **Not using the Management Reports feature for formal presentations.** Emailing a plain PDF export to a banker or investor looks amateur compared to a properly formatted management report package with a cover page and executive summary.
- **Granting accountant access with owner-level permissions unnecessarily.** When inviting your accountant, give them accountant-level access (not admin access) unless there is a specific reason they need full administrative control.
- **Setting up recurring emails and never verifying they're being delivered.** After setting up a scheduled report email, send yourself a test and check your spam folder. Confirm with recipients that they're receiving the reports as expected.

## Practice Exercise

Choose your most important financial report — most likely the Profit & Loss. Run it for the current year-to-date with a comparison to the prior year. Export it as a PDF and verify the formatting looks professional. Then go to Management Reports and create a simple two-report package combining the P&L and Balance Sheet. Add a cover page with your company name and a brief one-paragraph executive summary describing overall financial performance for the period. Export the package as a PDF. This is the document format you would share with a banker or investor.

## Key Takeaways

- QuickBooks reports can be exported as PDF (for sharing and archiving), Excel/CSV (for further analysis), or printed directly from the browser.
- The Email feature lets you send any report as a PDF attachment directly from within QuickBooks without downloading and re-attaching files.
- Management Reports creates polished, branded report packages combining multiple financial statements with cover pages, tables of contents, and custom text — ideal for bankers, boards, and investors.
- Sharing with your accountant is best done by inviting them as an Accounting Firm user, giving them direct access to your books rather than relying on manual report exports.
- Recurring scheduled emails (set up via the Custom Reports tab) automate routine report distribution so the right people always have current financial information without manual effort.
