# Users, Roles, and Permissions

Controlling who can see and do what in your QuickBooks account isn't just a security measure — it's how you protect your business finances, maintain accurate records, and give your team the access they need without exposing sensitive data.

## What You'll Learn

- Why access control matters for financial security and data integrity
- The different user types available in QuickBooks Online and what each one can do
- How to invite a new user and assign the right role
- How to set custom permissions for standard users
- How to remove a user's access when they leave your company
- How accountant access works and why it's different from a regular user

---

## Why Access Control Matters

Financial data is among the most sensitive information a business holds. An employee with unrestricted access could view owner compensation, see profit margins you consider confidential, or — in a worst case — create fraudulent transactions.

Good access controls also protect data accuracy. A sales employee who accidentally opens the reconciliation screen and confirms transactions doesn't need that ability. A part-time bookkeeper who only needs to enter expenses doesn't need to see payroll. Giving people only the access they need keeps your books cleaner and reduces the risk of costly errors.

There's also a practical business reason: QuickBooks Online limits the number of users based on your subscription plan. Simple Start allows 1 user, Essentials allows 3, Plus allows 5, and Advanced allows up to 25. Understanding user types helps you use those seats efficiently.

## QuickBooks Online User Types

QuickBooks Online has five distinct user roles. Each one is designed for a different business need.

### 1. Master Administrator
The Master Admin has unrestricted access to everything in QuickBooks, including billing, subscriptions, and all financial data. There is only one Master Admin per company.

- Full access to all transactions, reports, settings, and user management
- The only user who can change the billing plan or cancel the subscription
- The only user who can transfer the Master Admin role to someone else
- Typically the business owner or the person who created the QBO account

**Best for:** Business owner, managing partner

### 2. Company Administrator
A Company Admin has the same level of access as the Master Admin for day-to-day operations — they can do everything except manage billing and transfer the Master Admin role.

- Full access to all transactions and reports
- Can add, edit, and remove other users
- Cannot change subscription or billing details

**Best for:** A trusted office manager or operations director who runs the books but isn't the owner

### 3. Standard User
Standard Users have access limited to specific areas you define when you set them up. This is the most flexible role — you control exactly what they can see and do.

Standard User permissions are divided into categories:
- **Customers & Sales** — Create and send invoices, record payments, manage customers
- **Vendors & Purchases** — Enter bills, record expenses, manage vendors
- **All** — Access to both customers/sales and vendors/purchases

For each of those areas, you can further restrict to:
- **All access** — Create, edit, delete, and void transactions
- **Limited access** — Create and edit only, no delete or void
- **No access** — Cannot access this area at all

You can also control whether a Standard User can see:
- **Reports** — Specific reports or no reports at all
- **Payroll** — If you have payroll set up, whether this user can see payroll data

**Best for:** Bookkeepers, accounts payable clerks, accounts receivable specialists, office assistants

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** When setting up a bookkeeper as a Standard User, give them All access to both Customers & Sales and Vendors & Purchases, plus access to reports. Do not give them access to payroll unless they are managing payroll directly. This lets them do their job fully while keeping sensitive compensation data separate.

### 4. Reports Only
Reports Only users can view and run reports but cannot view individual transactions, enter data, or access settings. They see summary numbers, not the underlying transaction detail.

- Can access most standard financial reports
- Cannot see individual transaction amounts in registers
- Cannot create or edit any transactions
- Cannot access settings or user management

**Best for:** Business partners who want to monitor financial performance, silent investors, board members of a nonprofit

### 5. Time Tracking Only
Time Tracking Only users can only log their time in QuickBooks. They cannot see any financial data, reports, or other transactions.

- Access limited to entering time entries against customers or projects
- Cannot see any financial information
- Ideal for hourly employees or contractors who need to submit time for billing

**Best for:** Employees or contractors who bill their time to client projects

## How to Invite a New User

Step-by-step to add a user to your QuickBooks Online company:

1. Click the **Gear icon** at the top right of the screen.
2. Under **Your Company**, select **Manage Users**.
3. Click the **Add user** button in the upper right.
4. Select the **user type** from the list (Standard User, Reports Only, Time Tracking Only, or Company Admin). Note: you cannot add a second Master Admin.
5. If you selected **Standard User**, configure their permissions:
   - Choose their access level for Customers & Sales (All, Limited, or None)
   - Choose their access level for Vendors & Purchases (All, Limited, or None)
   - Set their Reports access (All reports, Limited reports, or None)
   - Toggle Payroll access on or off
6. Click **Next**.
7. Enter the user's **first name**, **last name**, and **email address**.
8. Click **Save**.

QuickBooks will send an invitation email to that address. The user must click the link in the email and either sign in with an existing Intuit account or create a new one. Until they accept the invitation, their status in **Manage Users** will show as "Invited."

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** If a user says they didn't receive the invitation email, have them check their spam folder. The email comes from intuit.com and is sometimes caught by spam filters. If it's still not found, go to Manage Users, find the user, and click the dropdown next to their name to select "Resend invitation."

## Editing User Permissions

User access needs change over time — an employee gets promoted, a role changes, or you want to tighten access after a security review. Here's how to update permissions:

1. Go to **Gear > Manage Users**.
2. Find the user and click the dropdown arrow on the right side of their row.
3. Select **Edit**.
4. Adjust their permissions as needed.
5. Click **Save**.

The user's access updates immediately — they don't need to log out and back in.

Note: You cannot change the Master Admin's access (since it's always full access), and you cannot demote a Company Admin to a Standard User while they have open tasks like pending payroll runs. Resolve those first.

---

## Removing a User's Access

When an employee leaves your company or a contractor engagement ends, remove their QuickBooks access the same day. Do not wait.

1. Go to **Gear > Manage Users**.
2. Find the user and click the dropdown arrow on the right side of their row.
3. Select **Delete**.
4. Confirm by clicking **Delete user**.

The user is immediately logged out of QuickBooks and can no longer access your company file. Their historical transactions (invoices, expenses they entered) remain in your books — you're only removing their login access, not their transaction history.

**Important:** If the departing user was your only Company Admin (other than you), make sure to designate a replacement before removing their access.

## Accountant Access: A Special User Type

Your accountant gets a different type of access called an **Accountant User**. This is separate from all the roles above and works differently:

- Your accountant uses **QuickBooks Online Accountant (QBOA)**, a free platform Intuit provides to accounting professionals.
- When you invite your accountant, they access your books through their QBOA account — they do not use one of your paid user seats.
- Accountant users have "super user" level access — they can see and edit everything, including areas that would be restricted for a Standard User. This is by design; accountants need full access to do their work.
- Your accountant can also put your books into **Accountant's Copy** mode to work on year-end adjustments without interfering with your ongoing transactions.

**To invite your accountant:**
1. Go to **My Accountant** in the left navigation menu.
2. Click **Invite your accountant**.
3. Enter your accountant's email address.
4. Click **Send invitation**.

Your accountant will receive the invitation in their QBOA dashboard. Once they accept, they can access your company file anytime — you don't need to send them a file or be online at the same time.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Making everyone a Company Admin for simplicity** — this eliminates your access controls entirely and exposes sensitive data to people who don't need it. Take the extra two minutes to set up proper Standard User roles.
- **Forgetting to remove access when someone leaves** — a former employee with active QuickBooks access is a real security risk. Make removing QuickBooks access part of your employee offboarding checklist.
- **Giving Reports Only access to your bookkeeper** — a bookkeeper needs to enter transactions; Reports Only prevents that. Match the role to the actual job responsibilities.
- **Not inviting your accountant through the official Accountant invite** — sharing your own login credentials with your accountant is a security risk and means you can't track who made which changes. Always use the formal invite process.
- **Ignoring pending invitations** — if a user never accepts their invitation, they still appear in your user list. Periodically review **Manage Users** and delete any invitations that have been pending for more than a week.

## Key Takeaways

- Access control protects your business from both external threats and accidental internal errors — never give users more access than they need to do their job.
- QuickBooks Online has five user types: Master Admin, Company Admin, Standard User, Reports Only, and Time Tracking Only — each designed for a different level of responsibility.
- Standard Users are the most flexible role; you can precisely control their access to sales, expenses, reports, and payroll.
- Accountant access is a separate user type that doesn't consume one of your paid user seats and gives your accountant the full access they need to support you.
- Remove QuickBooks access immediately when an employee or contractor leaves — make it a standard part of your offboarding process.
