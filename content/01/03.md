# Navigating the Dashboard

QuickBooks Online's dashboard is your command center — once you know what everything does, you'll move through your daily bookkeeping tasks with confidence and speed.

## What You'll Learn

- How the left navigation menu is organized and what each section contains
- What the home dashboard widgets show and how to read them at a glance
- How to use the + New button to create any transaction in seconds
- How to search for transactions, customers, vendors, and accounts
- Where to find recent transactions and your activity feed
- How to access settings using the gear icon
- Essential keyboard shortcuts that speed up your workflow

## The Big Picture: How QBO Is Organized

QuickBooks Online is built around a left-side navigation menu. Everything you need — entering transactions, running reports, managing customers, and changing settings — flows from that menu. The main dashboard (the home screen) gives you a visual summary of your financial health. Think of the dashboard as your morning briefing and the left menu as your filing cabinet.

When you log in, you land on the home dashboard. This is intentional — QuickBooks wants you to see your key numbers first before you dive into data entry.

## The Left Navigation Menu: Section by Section

The left navigation bar contains every major section of QuickBooks Online. Here's what each item does:

**Dashboard** — Returns you to the home screen with your financial snapshot widgets.

**Banking** — Connect your bank and credit card accounts here. This is where you'll review and categorize downloaded transactions. Sub-items include Bank Transactions and Rules.

**Sales** — Everything related to money coming in. Includes Invoices, Estimates, Customers, Products & Services, and your Sales Overview report.

**Expenses** — Everything related to money going out. Includes Expenses (individual transactions), Vendors, and Purchase Orders.

**Projects** — Track income and costs by job or project (available on Plus and Advanced plans).

**Payroll** — Manage employee pay, taxes, and filings (requires a payroll subscription).

**Reports** — Access all of QuickBooks' pre-built financial reports. The most commonly used are Profit & Loss, Balance Sheet, and Accounts Receivable Aging.

**Taxes** — Manage sales tax, payroll tax, and your QuickBooks-connected tax filings.

**Mileage** — Log business mileage for tax deductions, either manually or via the mobile app's GPS tracking.

**Accounting** — Your Chart of Accounts, Reconcile tool, and Journal Entries live here.

**My Accountant** — Share your books with your accountant, send them messages, and view their recommendations.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** You can collapse the left navigation menu by clicking the small arrow at the bottom left of the panel. This gives you more screen real estate when you're working in a wide report or reconciliation screen. Click it again to expand.

## The Home Dashboard Widgets: Reading Your Financial Pulse

The dashboard displays a series of widgets — visual summaries of key financial data. Understanding each one helps you spot problems early.

**Profit & Loss (P&L) Snapshot**
Shows your income and expenses for the current month (or a selected period). The bar chart compares income (green) to expenses (red). If the red bar is taller, you're spending more than you're earning in that period — a signal to investigate.

**Invoices**
Displays the total value of invoices in different stages: Overdue, Unpaid, and Paid in the last 30 days. Clicking any segment takes you directly to that filtered invoice list. This widget is your accounts receivable at a glance.

**Expenses**
Shows your total spending for the current period broken down by category. Hover over any slice of the pie chart to see the exact amount and category name. This helps you quickly see if any expense category is running unexpectedly high.

**Bank Accounts**
Lists your connected bank and credit card accounts with their current balances and how many transactions are waiting to be reviewed. A high number of unreviewed transactions is a reminder to spend time in the Banking section.

**Sales**
A bar chart showing total sales over recent months. Useful for spotting seasonal trends or a sudden drop in revenue.

You can rearrange and customize which widgets appear on your dashboard by clicking the **Edit** button (pencil icon) at the top right of the dashboard.

## The + New Button: Your Transaction Shortcut

The green **+ New** button at the top of the left navigation menu is how you create almost every type of transaction in QuickBooks. Click it and a menu expands with options organized into four columns:

**Customers** — Invoice, Estimate, Credit Memo, Sales Receipt, Refund Receipt, Delayed Credit, Delayed Charge

**Vendors** — Expense, Check, Bill, Pay Bills, Purchase Order, Vendor Credit, Credit Card Credit, Print Checks

**Employees** — Payroll (if enabled), Single Time Activity, Weekly Timesheet

**Other** — Bank Deposit, Transfer, Journal Entry, Statement, Inventory Qty Adjustment, Pay Down Credit Card

You'll use the + New button dozens of times every week. Muscle memory for this button is one of the first habits to build.

## Search: Finding Anything Quickly

The **magnifying glass icon** at the top right of the screen opens QuickBooks' global search. This is one of the most underused features in QBO — and one of the most powerful.

You can search by:
- Customer name (e.g., "Smith Landscaping")
- Invoice number (e.g., "INV-1042")
- Dollar amount (e.g., "847.50")
- Transaction type (e.g., "expense")
- Vendor name (e.g., "Staples")

Search results show a list of matching transactions. Click any result to open it directly. This is far faster than navigating through menus when you know what you're looking for.

To access **Advanced Search**, click "Advanced Search" at the bottom of the search results. Advanced Search lets you filter by date range, transaction type, amount range, and status — useful when you remember roughly when a transaction happened but not its exact number.

## The Gear Icon: Settings and Company Management

The **gear icon** at the top right of the screen (next to the search bar) is your gateway to all settings and administrative tools. Click it to access:

**Your Company**
- Account and Settings — billing plan, company info, sales settings, expense settings, and advanced options
- Manage Users — add, edit, or remove user access
- Custom Form Styles — customize the look of your invoices and estimates
- Chart of Accounts — quick link to your account list
- QuickBooks Labs — experimental features you can opt into

**Lists**
- All Lists — access product/service lists, payment methods, terms, and more
- Products and Services — manage what you sell

**Tools**
- Import Data — import customers, vendors, transactions from Excel or CSV
- Export Data — download your data
- Reconcile — access the bank reconciliation tool
- Budgeting — create and compare budgets

**Profile**
- User Profile — manage your Intuit account
- Feedback — submit feedback to Intuit
- Privacy — manage your data sharing settings

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Bookmark the path **Gear > Account and Settings > Advanced** — this is where you control your accounting method, fiscal year, chart of accounts preferences, and several other settings that affect how QuickBooks behaves across your entire file.

## Keyboard Shortcuts That Save Time

QuickBooks Online supports keyboard shortcuts that help you move faster without taking your hands off the keyboard. Press **Ctrl + Alt + ?** (Windows) or **Ctrl + Option + ?** (Mac) to view the full shortcut list inside QBO. The most useful ones:

| Action | Windows | Mac |
|---|---|---|
| Open + New menu | Ctrl + Alt + C | Ctrl + Option + C |
| Go to Invoices | Ctrl + Alt + I | Ctrl + Option + I |
| Go to Expenses | Ctrl + Alt + E | Ctrl + Option + E |
| Open Search | Ctrl + F | Ctrl + F |
| Go to Help | Ctrl + Alt + H | Ctrl + Option + H |
| Go to Reports | Ctrl + Alt + R | Ctrl + Option + R |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Ignoring the Banking widget's unreviewed transaction count** — Transactions your bank feeds into QuickBooks are not automatically categorized correctly. Letting them pile up means your P&L and expense reports are inaccurate until you review them.
- **Using the browser back button inside QBO** — This can cause data loss if you're mid-entry on a form. Always use QuickBooks' own navigation buttons and links.
- **Not using the + New button and instead hunting through menus** — The + New button is the fastest path to any transaction type. Learn it early.
- **Overlooking the Invoices widget** — This is your early warning system for overdue receivables. Check it every time you log in.
- **Skipping the search bar and scrolling through lists** — QuickBooks' search is fast and accurate. Use it instead of scrolling through long customer or transaction lists.

## Practice Exercise

Log into your QuickBooks Online account and complete the following navigation exercises without referring back to these notes:

1. From the dashboard, identify how many invoices are currently overdue.
2. Click the **+ New** button and locate where you would create a Bill (vendor invoice you owe). Don't create one — just find it.
3. Use the **Search** bar to search for any customer or vendor name in your company. Click one result to open that record.
4. Open the **Gear icon** and navigate to **Account and Settings > Advanced**. Note what your current accounting method and fiscal year start are set to.
5. Press **Ctrl + Alt + ?** (Windows) or **Ctrl + Option + ?** (Mac) to open the keyboard shortcuts panel. Find two shortcuts you'll use regularly.

## Key Takeaways

- The left navigation menu organizes all of QuickBooks into logical sections — Banking, Sales, Expenses, Reports, and Accounting are the areas you'll use most often.
- Dashboard widgets give you a real-time financial snapshot; check them each time you log in to catch problems early.
- The + New button is the fastest way to create any transaction — invoices, expenses, bills, checks, and more are all one click away.
- The global search bar finds any transaction, customer, or vendor by name, number, or amount — use it instead of scrolling.
- The gear icon gives you access to all company settings, user management, and administrative tools.
