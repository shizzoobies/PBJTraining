# Accounts Payable Aging Report

The Accounts Payable Aging Report is one of the most powerful tools in QuickBooks for understanding what your business owes, who it owes it to, and how urgently action is needed.

## What You'll Learn

- What the AP Aging Report shows and why it matters for your business
- How to run the AP Aging Summary and Detail reports in QuickBooks
- How to read the aging columns (Current, 1-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+ days)
- How to use the report to prioritize which bills to pay first
- How to plan cash flow and schedule payments to avoid late fees and damaged vendor relationships

---

## What Is the AP Aging Report?

"Accounts payable aging" sounds technical, but the concept is simple: it's a snapshot of every unpaid bill your business has, organized by how long it's been sitting unpaid.

The word "aging" refers to how old the debt is. A bill that's one week past due is different from a bill that's 60 days overdue — and your report shows you both at a glance, bucketed by how far past due they are.

Why does this matter?

- **Cash management**: You can see your total outstanding obligations before deciding how much to spend elsewhere.
- **Vendor relationships**: Paying late erodes trust with suppliers and can lead to stricter terms, credit holds, or lost business.
- **Avoiding late fees**: Many vendor invoices include late payment penalties. The aging report shows you what's at risk.
- **Audit readiness**: A clean AP aging confirms that all vendor invoices are properly recorded and nothing is sitting in limbo.

Think of the AP Aging Report as your bill-payment to-do list, ranked by urgency.

## Running the AP Aging Reports

QuickBooks offers two versions of the report — a Summary and a Detail view. You'll use both for different purposes.

**Steps to run the AP Aging Summary:**

1. In the left navigation, click **Reports**.
2. In the search bar at the top of the Reports page, type **Accounts Payable Aging** and press Enter.
3. You'll see two options: **A/P Aging Summary** and **A/P Aging Detail**. Click **A/P Aging Summary** first.
4. At the top of the report, set the **Report date** — this is the "as of" date, typically today or the end of a recent month.
5. The **Aging period** field controls how many days wide each column is. The default is 30 days, which gives you the standard Current / 1-30 / 31-60 / 61-90 / 91+ columns.
6. Click **Run Report** (or the report will auto-generate).
7. Review the results.

**Steps to run the AP Aging Detail:**

1. From the same Reports search, click **A/P Aging Detail**.
2. Set the same report date.
3. Click **Run Report**.
4. This version shows every individual bill rather than just vendor totals — you can see the bill number, due date, and amount for each open transaction.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Run the AP Aging Detail and sort by the **Past Due** column to surface the most overdue bills immediately. This is your action list — start calling vendors and scheduling payments from the top of this sorted list.

## Reading the Aging Columns

The AP Aging Report organizes all unpaid bills into columns based on how many days past due they are. Here's what each column means:

| Column | Meaning | Action Level |
|---|---|---|
| **Current** | Bills that are not yet due | Plan ahead — schedule payment |
| **1–30 days** | 1 to 30 days past the due date | Pay soon — low risk but action needed |
| **31–60 days** | 31 to 60 days past due | Urgent — contact vendor if you haven't |
| **61–90 days** | 61 to 90 days past due | Critical — vendor may halt service or add fees |
| **91 and over** | More than 90 days overdue | Severe — collections risk, vendor relationship at risk |

**Reading a row:**

Each row represents one vendor. Across the columns, you can see where their unpaid bills fall. For example:

- **City Office Rentals**: $1,200 in Current, $0 elsewhere — not yet due, no action needed today.
- **Acme Supplies Co.**: $0 Current, $340 in 1-30, $500 in 31-60 — this vendor has two overdue bills totaling $840. Pay the 31-60 day bill first.
- **Tech Solutions LLC**: $0 Current, $0 in 1-30, $0 in 31-60, $1,500 in 91+ — this bill is severely overdue and needs immediate attention.

The **Total** column on the right shows the vendor's complete outstanding balance across all aging buckets.

## Using the Report to Prioritize Payments

When cash is tight, you can't always pay every bill at once. The AP Aging Report helps you make smart decisions about what to pay first.

**A practical payment prioritization framework:**

1. **Pay overdue bills with late fees or penalties first.** If a vendor charges 1.5% monthly on past-due balances, that's real money leaving your pocket every day you wait.
2. **Pay vendors who supply critical goods or services.** Your electricity provider, your primary supplier, and your internet company should be kept current — disruption from these affects your ability to operate.
3. **Communicate with vendors in the 31-60+ day columns.** A quick phone call or email explaining a short delay goes a long way. Many vendors will work with you if you're proactive and honest.
4. **Preserve vendors who offer early payment discounts.** If a vendor offers 2% off for early payment, prioritize them when cash allows — the savings compound over time.
5. **Deprioritize current bills while resolving overdue ones.** If something isn't due yet and you're short on cash, it can wait until you've cleared the past-due column.

## Cash Flow Planning with AP Aging

The AP Aging Report is not just a history of what you owe — it's a planning tool for the weeks ahead. Use it alongside your expected income to map out your cash position.

**Steps for a monthly cash flow planning session using AP Aging:**

1. Run the AP Aging Summary as of today.
2. Note the **total Current** amount — these are bills coming due in the next 30 days.
3. Compare that total to your **available bank balance** and your **expected receivables** (money owed to you by customers) over the same period.
4. If expected cash inflows exceed your Current AP, you're in good shape. If not, you need to either delay some payments (with vendor communication) or pursue collections on outstanding customer invoices.
5. Make a **payment schedule**: list each bill, its due date, and the amount. Plan which bills you'll pay on which days to avoid overdrafts and keep accounts current.
6. Revisit the aging report weekly — not just monthly — if your business has high transaction volume or tight cash flow.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Use the **Memorize Report** feature to save your AP Aging configuration (date range, aging period, columns) so you can re-run it in one click. Go to the top of the report and click the gear icon, then **Memorize**. Name it something like "Weekly AP Review" and it will appear in your Memorized Reports list.

## Scheduling Bill Payments to Avoid Late Fees

Reactive bill paying — scrambling when a vendor calls — is stressful and expensive. A proactive payment schedule built around your AP Aging Report puts you in control.

**Best practices for scheduling payments:**

- **Pay on a fixed schedule** — twice a month is a common cadence for small businesses. Choose two specific days (e.g., the 5th and the 20th) and only pay bills on those days. This batches your work and makes cash flow more predictable.
- **Use QuickBooks' Pay Bills screen** on your payment days. Sort by due date and pay everything that's due before your next payment run date.
- **Set up vendor reminders** — if a vendor allows autopay, consider enrolling for small recurring bills (subscriptions, utilities). Record these in QuickBooks when they post to your bank feed.
- **Build a 30-day buffer** for new vendors — if possible, try to build a cash reserve equal to one month of AP obligations. This cushion lets you handle a slow revenue week without missing payments.
- **Review the 91+ column monthly** — if anything is ever in this column, treat it as an emergency. Call the vendor the same day you see it.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Running the report but not acting on it.** The AP Aging Report is only useful if you use it to take action. Schedule time each week to review and respond.
- **Using the wrong report date.** If you run the report with a date in the past, the aging will reflect that past date — not today's true balances. Always use today's date for operational decisions.
- **Ignoring the 91+ column.** Bills this old can trigger collection actions, damage your credit with vendors, or result in being cut off from supply. Treat anything 60+ days old as urgent.
- **Entering bills with wrong due dates.** If you entered Net 30 terms but the bill shows as Current forever, check whether the bill date and terms were entered correctly — errors here make the aging report misleading.
- **Not investigating why a balance sits in AP for months.** If a bill has been open for 90 days and you're sure you paid it, the payment may have been recorded incorrectly. Investigate rather than letting it age further.

## Practice Exercise

Practice reading and acting on an AP Aging Report:

1. Enter three open bills in QuickBooks with the following details (use past dates to create aging):
   - "Metro Utilities": $180, due 45 days ago — categorized to Utilities
   - "PrintFast Co.": $620, due 15 days ago — categorized to Advertising
   - "DataSafe Backup": $95, due in 10 days (not yet overdue) — categorized to Computer & Internet
2. Run the **AP Aging Summary** report as of today. Confirm Metro Utilities appears in the 31-60 column, PrintFast in the 1-30 column, and DataSafe in Current.
3. Run the **AP Aging Detail** report and sort by the due date column.
4. Based on the report, decide which bill to pay first. Then go to **+ New > Pay Bills** and pay Metro Utilities in full.
5. Re-run the AP Aging Summary and confirm Metro Utilities no longer appears.

## Key Takeaways

- The AP Aging Report organizes all unpaid bills by how many days past due they are — Current, 1-30, 31-60, 61-90, and 91+ days.
- Run the AP Aging Summary for a high-level vendor balance overview; use the Detail report to see individual bills and drill into specific transactions.
- Use the report as a weekly action list: bills in the 31+ day columns need immediate attention to protect vendor relationships and avoid late fees.
- Pair the AP Aging Report with your expected cash inflows to build a realistic payment schedule — this is the foundation of basic cash flow management.
- Memorize the report configuration in QuickBooks so you can re-run it in one click during your regular payment review sessions.
