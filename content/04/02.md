# Purchase Orders

A purchase order is one of the most underused tools in QuickBooks — but for businesses that buy inventory or order goods from vendors, it's a powerful way to control spending and track what's been received.

## What You'll Learn

- What a purchase order is and when your business should use one
- How to turn on the purchase order feature in QuickBooks settings
- How to create, send, and manage purchase orders
- How to receive items against a PO, including partial receipts
- How to close a purchase order when it's fulfilled

---

## What Is a Purchase Order and When Should You Use One?

A purchase order (PO) is a document you send to a vendor that says, in writing: "We want to buy this specific thing, in this quantity, at this price." It's a formal commitment — but critically, it is **not** a payment. No money leaves your account when you create a PO.

Purchase orders are most useful when:

- You order physical inventory or goods that need to be received and inspected before payment
- You want to track orders that are in-progress (sent but not yet received)
- You need to give vendors a reference number they can put on their invoice
- You want managers to approve purchases before a bill is entered
- Your business orders from the same vendors repeatedly and you want a paper trail

If you pay for services immediately (a one-time repair, a monthly subscription, a contractor invoice), you likely don't need a PO — use an Expense or Bill instead.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Even if you're a small business, using POs for large or recurring orders gives you a built-in way to catch vendor billing errors. When the bill arrives, you compare it to the PO — if the amounts don't match, you have documented proof of what was agreed.

## Enabling Purchase Orders in QuickBooks

Purchase orders are not turned on by default in QuickBooks Online. You need to enable them in settings before you'll see the option.

**To enable purchase orders:**

1. Click the **Settings gear icon** in the top-right corner of QuickBooks.
2. Select **Account and Settings** (or "Company Settings" in some versions).
3. Click on the **Expenses** tab in the left column.
4. Find the **Purchase Orders** section.
5. Toggle the switch to turn **Use purchase orders** on.
6. Optionally, set a **default message to vendors** that will appear on every PO you send (e.g., "Please reference this PO number on your invoice.").
7. If you want to add a custom PO number prefix (like "PO-" or your company initials), enter it in the **Custom transaction numbers** field.
8. Click **Save**, then **Done**.

Once enabled, **Purchase Order** will appear as an option when you click **+ New** in the left navigation.

## Creating a Purchase Order

Now that POs are enabled, here's how to create one from scratch.

**Steps to create a purchase order:**

1. Click **+ New** in the left navigation panel.
2. Under the **Vendors** column, select **Purchase Order**.
3. In the **Vendor** dropdown, select the vendor you're ordering from. If they're not in your list, click **+ Add new**.
4. Set the **Ship To** field — choose your business address or a specific location if you have multiple sites.
5. Enter the **Purchase Order Date** (today's date is filled automatically).
6. Enter the **Expected delivery date** so you can track whether the order arrives on time.
7. In the line items table, click in the **Product/Service** column and select the item you're ordering. If you track inventory, use the specific inventory item. For non-inventory goods, you can type a description.
8. Enter the **Quantity** and confirm the **Rate** (price per unit). QuickBooks will calculate the **Amount** automatically.
9. Add additional line items as needed by clicking **Add a line**.
10. In the **Message to vendor** field, add any special instructions (delivery instructions, reference numbers, etc.).
11. Review the **Total** at the bottom right.
12. Click **Save and send** to email the PO directly to the vendor, or **Save and close** to save it without sending.

## Sending the Purchase Order to Your Vendor

QuickBooks makes it easy to email POs directly to your vendor from inside the app.

When you click **Save and send**, QuickBooks will open an email preview window showing:

- The **To** email address (pulled from the vendor record — make sure it's accurate)
- A **subject line** you can customize
- The **PO as a PDF attachment**

You can edit the email message before sending. The vendor receives the PO as a professional-looking PDF with your business name, address, and PO number.

If you prefer to download and send manually, click **Print or Preview** to save the PO as a PDF and attach it to your own email.

## Receiving Items Against a Purchase Order

When goods arrive from the vendor, you need to record the receipt in QuickBooks. This is called "receiving against a PO," and it converts the PO into a bill or expense.

**To receive items on a PO:**

1. Go to **Expenses > Vendors** and click the vendor's name.
2. Find the open purchase order in the transaction list and click it to open.
3. Notice the green **Create bill** button (or **Create expense**, depending on your settings) — click it.
4. QuickBooks will open a Bill form that's pre-filled with all the information from the PO: vendor, items, quantities, and rates.
5. Review the bill for accuracy. If the vendor's invoice matches the PO exactly, you're done — just add the **Bill date** and **Due date** from the vendor's invoice.
6. In the **PO number** field or on the bill, confirm the PO reference is linked.
7. Click **Save and close**.

The purchase order will now show a status of **Closed** (if fully received) or **Open** (if partially received).

## Handling Partial Receipts

Sometimes a vendor ships part of your order immediately and the rest later. QuickBooks handles this gracefully.

When you create a bill from a PO and change the quantity to less than what was ordered:

1. Open the PO and click **Create bill**.
2. In the bill's line items, **reduce the quantity** to reflect only what actually arrived.
3. Save the bill.
4. The PO status will remain **Open** with the remaining quantity still outstanding.
5. When the second shipment arrives, repeat the process — open the PO, create another bill, and enter the remaining quantities.
6. Once all items have been received and billed, the PO closes automatically.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** If a partial shipment arrives and you're not sure when the rest is coming, add a note in the PO's **Memo** field with the expected date of the remaining items. This helps you follow up with the vendor without digging through emails.

## Closing a Purchase Order Manually

Sometimes a PO needs to be closed without being fully received — maybe you cancelled part of the order, or the vendor is out of stock and you've decided not to wait.

**To manually close a PO:**

1. Open the purchase order (Expenses > Vendors > click vendor > click the PO).
2. Click **Edit** if the form isn't already editable.
3. In the **Status** dropdown at the top of the form, change it from **Open** to **Closed**.
4. Add a note in the **Memo** field explaining why (e.g., "Cancelled remaining 5 units — item discontinued").
5. Click **Save and close**.

Closing a PO manually tells QuickBooks not to expect more bills against it, which keeps your open PO report clean.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Forgetting to enable purchase orders in settings** — if you don't see PO as an option under + New, this is the reason.
- **Creating a PO but never converting it to a bill** — the vendor's invoice will never get recorded, and your expenses will be understated.
- **Changing quantities on the bill without updating the PO** — always work from the PO when receiving goods so the two stay linked.
- **Using a PO for service-based expenses** — POs work best for goods and inventory. For services, use a bill or expense directly.
- **Leaving old POs open indefinitely** — review your open PO report monthly and close any orders that are complete or cancelled.

## Key Takeaways

- Purchase orders are commitments to buy — they are not payments and do not affect your cash or bank balance until a bill is created from them.
- You must enable purchase orders in Account and Settings > Expenses before they appear as an option.
- Always convert a received PO into a bill so the vendor's invoice is properly recorded in your books.
- Partial receipts keep the PO open with the remaining quantity, allowing for additional bills as shipments arrive.
- Close stale or cancelled purchase orders manually to keep your open PO report accurate and actionable.
