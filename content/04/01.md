# Setting Up and Managing Vendors

Your vendor list is the foundation of everything in accounts payable — keeping it clean and accurate saves you time, prevents costly mistakes, and makes tax season far less painful.

## What You'll Learn

- Why maintaining a clean vendor list matters for your business finances
- How to add a new vendor with all the required information
- How to configure vendor defaults like payment terms and expense accounts
- How to set up 1099 tracking for contractors
- How to merge duplicate vendors and deactivate vendors you no longer use

---

## Why Your Vendor List Matters

Every bill you enter, every check you write, and every 1099 you file at year-end ties back to a vendor record. If your vendor list is messy — full of duplicates, missing addresses, or mismatched names — you'll run into problems when it's time to pay bills, reconcile accounts, or file taxes.

A well-maintained vendor list gives you:

- **Accurate aging reports**: You can see exactly what you owe and to whom.
- **Faster bill entry**: Default settings auto-fill common fields so you spend less time typing.
- **Clean 1099 reporting**: QuickBooks can only generate 1099s for properly configured vendors.
- **Better audit readiness**: Clean records protect you if you're ever questioned by the IRS.

Think of your vendor list as your business's contact book for everyone you pay — treat it with the same care you'd give your customer list.

## Adding a New Vendor

Adding vendors correctly from the start prevents the most common accounts payable headaches. Here's how to do it right.

**Steps to add a new vendor:**

1. From the left navigation menu, click **Expenses**, then select **Vendors**.
2. In the top-right corner, click the green **New Vendor** button.
3. In the **Display name as** field, enter the name exactly as you want it to appear on checks and reports. Use the company name for businesses; use "Last, First" format for individuals (e.g., "Garcia, Maria").
4. Enter the vendor's **full mailing address** — this is required for printing checks and filing 1099s.
5. Add the vendor's **email address** and **phone number** for your reference and for sending purchase orders.
6. Under **Payment Terms**, set the default terms (e.g., Net 30, Due on Receipt). QuickBooks will auto-apply these to every bill you enter for this vendor.
7. In the **Account no.** field, enter your customer account number with this vendor (if applicable). This prints on check stubs, which helps vendors apply payments faster.
8. Under **Default expense account**, choose the account this vendor's bills should hit most often. For example, set your internet provider's default to "Internet & Telephone."
9. If this vendor is a contractor or freelancer who may need a 1099, check the box that says **Track payments for 1099**.
10. Click **Save** when finished.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Use the **Notes** field to store important vendor details — contract renewal dates, preferred contact person, or notes about payment preferences. This information is visible only internally and can save you from digging through emails later.

## Required Fields and Best Practices

QuickBooks won't prevent you from saving a vendor with minimal information, but skimping on setup creates work later. Here are the fields that matter most:

**Display Name**: This is how the vendor appears in every dropdown, report, and document. Be consistent — don't add "Home Depot" in one entry and "The Home Depot #3421" in another.

**Address**: Required for printing mailed checks and generating 1099 forms. If you pay electronically, you still need this for 1099 compliance.

**Payment Terms**: Setting accurate terms means your AP aging report will reflect real due dates, not just the date you entered the bill. Common options: Net 15, Net 30, Net 60, Due on Receipt.

**Tax ID (EIN or SSN)**: For vendors you'll pay $600 or more in a year, you'll need their Tax ID number to file a 1099. You can store this in the **Business ID No.** field. Always collect a W-9 from contractors before you pay them.

**Default Expense Account**: This saves time on repetitive bill entry. If your landlord's bills always go to "Rent Expense," set that as the default and QuickBooks fills it in automatically.

## Setting Up 1099 Tracking

If you hire contractors, freelancers, or any non-employee and pay them $600 or more in a calendar year, you're required to file a 1099-NEC with the IRS. QuickBooks can help you track and file these — but only if the vendor is set up correctly.

To enable 1099 tracking for a vendor:

1. Open the vendor's record (Expenses > Vendors > click vendor name).
2. Click **Edit** in the top-right corner.
3. Scroll down to find the checkbox labeled **Track payments for 1099**.
4. Check the box.
5. Make sure the vendor's legal name, address, and Tax ID (EIN or SSN) are filled in.
6. Click **Save**.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Don't wait until January to set up 1099 tracking. If you add this checkbox to a vendor mid-year, QuickBooks will only track payments made after the box was checked — it will not retroactively capture earlier payments in the 1099 workflow. Check your vendor list quarterly and fix any gaps early.

## Merging Duplicate Vendors

Duplicate vendors happen — someone adds "Office Depot" and someone else adds "Office Depot Inc." before you notice. Merging them keeps your records clean and rolls all transactions into a single record.

**To merge two vendors:**

1. Go to **Expenses > Vendors**.
2. Open the vendor record you want to **keep** and note the exact name spelling.
3. Open the duplicate vendor record and click **Edit**.
4. Change the **Display Name** to exactly match the vendor you want to keep (character for character, including spacing and capitalization).
5. Click **Save**. QuickBooks will warn you that a vendor with that name already exists and ask if you want to merge.
6. Click **Yes** to confirm the merge.

All transactions from the duplicate vendor will now appear under the surviving vendor record. This cannot be undone, so double-check before confirming.

## Making Vendors Inactive

When you stop working with a vendor, you don't want to delete them — that would erase your transaction history. Instead, make them inactive. Inactive vendors disappear from dropdowns and search results but remain in your records.

1. Go to **Expenses > Vendors**.
2. Find the vendor, click the small arrow or checkbox next to their name.
3. From the **Action** dropdown on the right, choose **Make inactive**.
4. Confirm when prompted.

To view inactive vendors later, click the **Settings gear** icon on the Vendors list and check **Include inactive**.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Adding the same vendor multiple times with slightly different names.** Search before adding — type the vendor name in the Vendors search bar first.
- **Skipping the address field.** Missing addresses will prevent you from printing checks and filing 1099s correctly.
- **Forgetting to check the 1099 box for contractors.** Once payments are made without the box checked, you'll need to manually track those amounts outside QuickBooks.
- **Using "miscellaneous" or "various" as a vendor name.** Every payee should have a real record so your reports are meaningful.
- **Deleting vendors instead of making them inactive.** Deletion removes the vendor from historical reports, which distorts your books.

## Key Takeaways

- A clean, complete vendor list is the foundation of accurate accounts payable and painless tax filing.
- Always fill in the display name, address, payment terms, and default expense account when adding a new vendor.
- The 1099 tracking checkbox must be enabled before payments are made — retroactive tracking requires manual workarounds.
- Use the merge function to consolidate duplicate vendors rather than deleting records.
- Deactivate vendors you no longer use instead of deleting them to preserve your transaction history.
