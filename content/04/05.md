# Managing Credit Cards in QuickBooks

Credit cards are one of the most common ways small businesses pay for expenses, and QuickBooks has a clean, structured workflow for tracking every charge, payment, and reconciliation.

## What You'll Learn

- How to set up a credit card as its own account in QuickBooks
- How to connect your credit card feed for automatic transaction import
- How to categorize credit card charges correctly
- How to record credit card payments to reduce your balance
- How to reconcile your credit card account just like a bank account
- How to handle credit card refunds and chargebacks

---

## Setting Up a Credit Card Account

In QuickBooks, a business credit card is treated as a **liability account** in your chart of accounts — because a credit card balance is money you owe, not money you have. Setting it up correctly is the first step.

**Steps to add a credit card account:**

1. Click the **Settings gear icon** in the top-right corner of QuickBooks.
2. Select **Chart of Accounts**.
3. Click the green **New** button in the top-right corner.
4. In the **Account Type** dropdown, select **Credit Card**.
5. In the **Detail Type** dropdown, select **Credit Card**.
6. Enter a clear **Name** for the account — use the card name and last four digits for easy identification (e.g., "Chase Ink Business - 4821").
7. Leave the **Description** blank or add a note if helpful.
8. Set the **Opening balance** only if you're starting mid-year and want to record the existing balance. If you're starting fresh, leave it at zero and enter all transactions manually from your last statement.
9. Click **Save and Close**.

The credit card now appears in your Chart of Accounts as a liability and is available as a payment method when entering expenses.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** If you have multiple business credit cards, set up a separate account for each one. Never lump all cards into a single account — you won't be able to reconcile individual statements, and your liability balance will be impossible to verify.

## Connecting Your Credit Card Feed

QuickBooks Online can connect directly to most major banks and credit card issuers, automatically downloading transactions so you don't have to type them in manually.

**Steps to connect a credit card bank feed:**

1. Go to **Bookkeeping** (or **Accounting**) in the left navigation, then select **Transactions > Bank transactions** (or "Banking").
2. Click **Connect account** or the **Add account** button.
3. Search for your credit card issuer by name (e.g., "Chase," "American Express," "Capital One").
4. Follow the prompts to log in with your online banking credentials. QuickBooks uses a secure, read-only connection — it can see your transactions but cannot move money.
5. Select the specific credit card account you want to connect.
6. Choose a **date range** for the initial import — typically 90 days back is a good starting point.
7. Map the connected card to the credit card account you created in the chart of accounts.
8. Click **Connect** and let the import run.

Once connected, QuickBooks downloads new credit card transactions automatically (usually daily). You'll then review and categorize them rather than entering them from scratch.

## Categorizing Credit Card Charges

When transactions download from your credit card feed, they appear in the **For Review** tab under Bank Transactions. Each one needs to be reviewed and categorized before it's added to your books.

**Steps to categorize downloaded credit card transactions:**

1. Go to **Bookkeeping > Transactions > Bank transactions**.
2. Click the **For Review** tab.
3. Filter by your credit card account using the dropdown at the top if you have multiple accounts connected.
4. Click on any transaction to expand it.
5. QuickBooks will suggest a category based on the merchant name and your past entries. Review the suggestion — if it's correct, click **Add** to accept.
6. If the category is wrong, click the **Category** dropdown and select the correct expense account.
7. Add a brief **Description** or **Memo** for your records.
8. If the charge relates to a specific customer or project, assign it in the **Customer** field.
9. To mark it as billable, check the **Billable** checkbox.
10. Click **Add** to post the transaction to your books, or **Match** if it matches a transaction you've already entered manually.

**Splitting a transaction:** If a single charge covers multiple expense categories (e.g., you bought office supplies and a business book in one Amazon order), click **Split** to divide the amount across two or more expense accounts.

## Recording Credit Card Payments

Making a payment toward your credit card balance is not an expense — it's a transfer from your bank account (an asset) to your credit card account (a liability). If you record it as an expense, you'll double-count the costs.

**Method 1: Using Transfer (recommended for manual entry)**

1. Click **+ New** in the left navigation.
2. Under the **Other** column, select **Transfer**.
3. In the **Transfer Funds From** dropdown, select your **checking or savings account**.
4. In the **Transfer Funds To** dropdown, select your **credit card account**.
5. Enter the **Amount** you paid (the minimum payment, a partial payment, or the full balance).
6. Set the **Date** to when the payment was made or will be made.
7. Add a memo if needed (e.g., "April credit card payment").
8. Click **Save and close**.

**Method 2: Matching from the bank feed**

When the credit card payment shows up as a debit on your checking account's bank feed, QuickBooks will often recognize it as a transfer and suggest a match to the credit card account. Review and confirm the match rather than categorizing it as an expense.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Never record a credit card payment using **+ New > Expense** with your checking account as the payment method. This creates an expense transaction that hits a P&L account, which is wrong — a credit card payment is a balance sheet transfer, not an operating expense.

## Reconciling Credit Cards

Credit card reconciliation works exactly like bank reconciliation — you compare QuickBooks' records against your monthly credit card statement to make sure every transaction is accounted for and the ending balance matches.

**Steps to reconcile a credit card account:**

1. Go to **Bookkeeping > Reconcile** (or find it under Accounting > Reconcile).
2. From the **Account** dropdown, select your credit card account.
3. Enter the **Ending balance** from your credit card statement.
4. Enter the **Statement ending date** (the last day of the billing period).
5. Click **Start reconciling**.
6. Check off each transaction that appears on your statement. Charges and credits should match line by line.
7. As you check items off, watch the **Difference** at the bottom right. Your goal is $0.00.
8. If the difference is not zero, you have either a missing transaction, a duplicate, or an amount that doesn't match. Investigate before finishing.
9. Once the difference hits $0.00, click **Finish now**.

Do this every month when your credit card statement arrives — don't let months pile up, as older errors become much harder to find.

## Handling Credit Card Refunds and Chargebacks

When a vendor refunds a credit card charge, the credit appears on your statement as a negative amount. Here's how to handle it correctly.

**Recording a vendor credit/refund:**

1. Click **+ New** in the left navigation.
2. Under the **Vendors** column, select **Vendor Credit**.
3. Select the **Vendor** who issued the refund.
4. Set the **Date** to when the refund was issued.
5. Enter the **Credit Amount**.
6. In the **Category** field, select the same expense account as the original charge (so it reverses correctly).
7. Click **Save and close**.

When the credit appears on your credit card statement and downloads through the bank feed, QuickBooks will suggest matching it to the vendor credit you created. Confirm the match to clear both records.

**Handling chargebacks:**

A chargeback is when your credit card company reverses a charge on your behalf (e.g., after a dispute). Record it the same way as a vendor credit — as a credit to the original expense account. If you ultimately lose the dispute and the charge is re-applied, enter it as a new expense.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Recording credit card payments as expenses.** This doubles the cost and makes your P&L inaccurate. Always use Transfer.
- **Forgetting to reconcile the credit card account.** An unreconciled credit card can hide duplicate charges, fraud, or missed credits for months.
- **Mixing personal and business charges on a business credit card.** If it happens, record the personal charge to an Owner's Draw account — don't leave it as a business expense.
- **Creating a new expense manually for every credit card charge while also downloading the feed.** This creates duplicates. Commit to one method: either enter manually and match the feed, or let the feed drive and just categorize.
- **Not setting up separate accounts for each card.** One account per card is the only way to reconcile accurately.

## Key Takeaways

- Credit cards are liability accounts in QuickBooks — every charge increases what you owe, and every payment reduces it.
- Connecting a credit card bank feed eliminates manual entry and speeds up categorization significantly.
- Credit card payments must be recorded as transfers, not expenses — recording them as expenses double-counts your costs.
- Reconcile your credit card monthly against the statement, the same way you reconcile your bank account.
- Vendor refunds and chargebacks are recorded as Vendor Credits, not as negative expenses, to keep your accounts clean.
