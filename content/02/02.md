# Using Bank Feeds to Import Transactions

Once your bank account is connected, the Bank Feeds feature becomes your daily command center for keeping your books current without tedious manual entry.

## What You'll Learn

- How to navigate the For Review tab and understand what you're looking at
- The difference between matching and adding transactions
- How QuickBooks suggests categories and why those suggestions aren't always right
- How to take batch actions on multiple transactions at once
- When and how to exclude transactions
- How to manually upload a CSV file when your bank isn't supported

## Understanding the For Review Tab

The **For Review** tab is where every imported bank transaction lands before it officially enters your books. Think of it as an inbox — transactions sit here waiting for your approval. Until you act on them, they do not appear in your Profit & Loss report, your Balance Sheet, or any other financial statement.

To get there: go to **Banking > Banking** (left navigation menu), then click the **For Review** tab at the top of the transaction list.

Each row in the For Review tab shows you:

- **Date** — the date the transaction posted to your bank
- **Description** — the raw text from your bank (often messy, like "SQ *OFFICE DEPOT 800-463-3768")
- **Payee** — QuickBooks' best guess at who the vendor is (editable)
- **Category** — QuickBooks' suggested account from your chart of accounts
- **Spent / Received** — the amount, showing whether money went out or came in

## Matching vs. Adding Transactions

This is one of the most important concepts in bank feeds. Every transaction you see in For Review will be handled in one of two ways:

**Match** — QuickBooks found an existing transaction already in your books that corresponds to this bank transaction. For example, if you previously created an invoice for $1,500 and your client just paid it, QuickBooks will see the $1,500 deposit and suggest it as a match to that open invoice. Matching links the two together and marks the invoice as paid. Always look for match suggestions before clicking Add.

**Add** — No existing transaction was found in QuickBooks, so this is a brand-new entry. Clicking Add records it directly into your books using whatever category and payee are shown (which you should review first).

To see whether a transaction has a match suggestion, click the transaction row to expand it. If QuickBooks found a possible match, you'll see a **Match** tab with the suggested existing transaction. If no match exists, you'll only see the **Add** option.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** If you pay bills by check and also have the bank connected, be careful not to accidentally "Add" a transaction that should be "Matched" to a bill you already entered. Double-matching means the expense gets recorded twice.

## How QuickBooks Suggests Categories

QuickBooks uses several signals to suggest categories for incoming transactions:

1. **Vendor recognition** — If you've categorized a transaction from "Amazon" as Office Supplies before, QuickBooks will suggest Office Supplies again for future Amazon transactions.
2. **Description keywords** — Certain words in the bank description trigger category suggestions (e.g., "fuel" or "gas" might suggest Automobile: Fuel).
3. **Amount patterns** — Recurring amounts to the same vendor reinforce category suggestions.
4. **Community data** — QuickBooks uses anonymized data from millions of businesses to suggest common categories for well-known vendors.

These suggestions are helpful but not always correct. A purchase at Costco could be office supplies, janitorial supplies, or food for a client meeting — QuickBooks doesn't know which. Always review the suggested category before approving.

## How to Review and Categorize a Single Transaction

1. In the **For Review** tab, click anywhere on a transaction row to expand it.
2. Review the **Payee** field. If it's blank or wrong, type the correct vendor name (e.g., "Staples" instead of "STPL*STAPLES 00112345").
3. Click the **Category** dropdown and select the correct account from your chart of accounts (e.g., Office Supplies, Meals & Entertainment, Utilities).
4. Add a **Memo** if needed — a brief note that will appear on reports (e.g., "Printer paper and toner for Q2").
5. If the transaction is a match to an existing entry, click the **Match** tab and confirm the match.
6. Click **Add** or **Match** to move the transaction out of For Review and into your books.

## Taking Batch Actions to Save Time

If you have dozens of similar transactions waiting — for example, 15 separate fuel purchases all needing the same category — you don't have to handle them one by one.

1. In the **For Review** tab, check the checkbox to the left of the first transaction.
2. Continue checking all similar transactions you want to handle together.
3. A blue action bar will appear at the top of the list. Click **Batch actions**.
4. Choose **Accept selected** to add all checked transactions using their current suggested categories, or **Categorize selected** to assign a single category to all of them at once.

Batch actions work best when you're confident the suggested category is correct, or when you're bulk-assigning the same category to many similar transactions. For transactions where the category might vary, review them individually.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Use the filter options at the top of the For Review tab to sort by amount or filter by date range. This makes it easy to find all transactions over $500 that need your attention, or to clear out a specific month before closing the books.

## Excluding Transactions

Sometimes a transaction in your bank feed should not be recorded in QuickBooks at all. Common examples:

- A transfer between two of your own business accounts (it will show up in both accounts' feeds)
- A personal expense that accidentally went through your business account
- A refund that reverses a transaction already handled another way

To exclude a transaction:

1. Check the checkbox next to the transaction.
2. Click **Batch actions > Exclude selected** (or click the transaction and choose Exclude from within the expanded view).

Excluded transactions move to the **Excluded** tab. They are not deleted from your bank feed — they're just set aside. You can un-exclude them later if needed.

**Important:** For inter-account transfers, use the **Transfer** option rather than excluding. In the category dropdown, choose **Transfer**, then select the destination account. This records the movement of money correctly without creating a phantom expense.

## Manually Uploading a CSV File

If your bank doesn't support a direct connection, you can download a transaction file from your bank's website and upload it manually. Here's how:

1. Log into your bank's online portal and navigate to your account history or statement download section.
2. Choose a date range (e.g., the current month) and download the file in **CSV**, **QBO**, **OFX**, or **QFX** format. QBO and OFX formats upload the cleanest; CSV may require column mapping.
3. In QuickBooks, go to **Banking > Banking**, click the **arrow next to Add account**, and select **Upload from file**.
4. Click **Browse** and locate the file you downloaded.
5. Select the QuickBooks account you want to import transactions into (e.g., your Business Checking account).
6. If you're uploading a CSV, QuickBooks will ask you to map columns — match the Date, Description, and Amount columns from your file to the correct fields.
7. Click **Next** and review the transactions QuickBooks found in the file.
8. Click **Import** to move the transactions into your For Review tab.

From there, the review and categorization process is identical to bank feed transactions.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Clicking Add without reviewing the category.** QuickBooks' suggestions are a starting point, not a guarantee. Always glance at the category before approving.
- **Ignoring the Match option.** If you've already recorded a bill or invoice and then accept the bank transaction as Add instead of Match, you'll double-count the transaction.
- **Adding bank transfers as expenses.** A transfer from your business checking to your business savings is not an expense. Use the Transfer category or it will inflate your reported expenses.
- **Letting For Review accumulate for months.** The longer you wait, the harder it becomes to remember what transactions were for. Review weekly at minimum.
- **Uploading the same CSV file twice.** QuickBooks may not always catch duplicate imports. Keep track of which date ranges you've uploaded to avoid recording transactions twice.

## Practice Exercise

Open your **For Review** tab and work through at least 10 transactions. For each one: confirm or correct the payee name, verify the suggested category, add a brief memo for any transaction over $100, and click Add or Match. If you have two transactions that clearly belong to the same category, try using the batch action feature to categorize them together. Note any transactions you're unsure about — we'll cover how to handle those in Lesson 2.3.

## Key Takeaways

- The For Review tab is your transaction inbox — nothing counts in your books until you approve it from here.
- Match links a bank transaction to an existing QuickBooks entry (like a paid invoice); Add creates a new entry.
- QuickBooks suggests categories based on past behavior and vendor recognition, but you should always verify before approving.
- Batch actions let you approve or categorize many similar transactions at once, saving significant time.
- CSV upload is a reliable fallback for banks without direct feed support — use QBO or OFX format when available for the cleanest import.
