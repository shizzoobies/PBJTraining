# Setting Up and Managing Customers

Your customer list is the foundation of your accounts receivable — if it's messy, everything downstream gets messy too.

## What You'll Learn

- Why a clean, well-organized customer list saves time and prevents errors
- How to add a new customer with all the right fields
- The difference between Display Name and Company Name
- How to set up billing and shipping addresses and payment terms
- How to merge duplicate customers and make old customers inactive

---

## Why Your Customer List Matters

Think of your customer list as your business's address book — but one that's directly tied to your invoices, payments, and reports. When customer records are incomplete or duplicated, problems multiply fast: invoices go to the wrong address, reports show the same customer twice, and payment terms get applied inconsistently.

A clean customer list means:

- Invoices reach the right person the first time
- You can pull accurate sales reports by customer
- Payment reminders go to the correct contact
- Your accounts receivable aging report is reliable

Taking 5 minutes to set up a customer correctly at the start saves hours of cleanup later.

## Adding a New Customer

QuickBooks makes it straightforward to add customers, but knowing which fields actually matter — and which ones you can skip — saves time.

**Navigation path:** Go to **Sales** in the left menu > click **Customers** > click the green **New Customer** button in the top right.

**Step-by-step:**

1. Click **Sales** in the left navigation panel.
2. Click **Customers** in the submenu that appears.
3. Click the **New Customer** button (top right, green).
4. In the **Customer Display Name** field, enter the name exactly as you want it to appear on invoices and in your list (e.g., "Smith Plumbing LLC").
5. Enter the **Company Name** if the customer is a business — this is the legal business name.
6. Add the primary **Email Address** — this is where invoices will be sent by default.
7. Add a **Phone** number.
8. Under the **Address** tab, enter the **Billing Address**. If the shipping address differs, uncheck "Same as billing address" and enter the shipping address separately.
9. Click the **Payment and Billing** tab — set the **Payment Terms** (e.g., Net 30) and preferred **Payment Method**.
10. If this customer has an existing balance from before you started using QuickBooks, enter it in the **Opening Balance** field along with the **Opening Balance Date**.
11. Click **Save**.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** The **Customer Display Name** is what appears on your invoices and customer-facing documents. Use a consistent format across all customers — either "First Last" for individuals or "Company Name" for businesses. Mixing formats makes your reports harder to read.

## Required vs. Optional Fields

You don't have to fill in every field to save a customer record, but skipping important fields causes problems later.

**Fields you should always fill in:**
- Customer Display Name (required by QuickBooks)
- Email address (needed to send invoices electronically)
- Billing address (needed for mailed invoices and tax purposes)
- Payment terms (controls due dates on invoices)

**Fields that are optional but useful:**
- Company name
- Phone number
- Shipping address (only needed if you ship physical goods)
- Tax ID / resale number (for sales tax exemptions)
- Notes (great for recording special instructions)

**Opening Balance:** Only use this field if you are setting up QuickBooks mid-year and the customer already owes you money from before you started. If you enter an opening balance, QuickBooks creates a journal entry to record it. Don't use this if you're going to enter historical invoices manually — doing both will double the balance.

## Customer Display Name vs. Company Name

This is one of the most common sources of confusion for new QuickBooks users.

- **Company Name** is the legal name of the business — what goes on contracts and official documents.
- **Customer Display Name** is how the customer appears in your QuickBooks list, on invoices, and in reports.

For most businesses, these can be identical. But sometimes you need them to differ. For example, if you work with "ABC Corporation" but your day-to-day contact is the "Purchasing Department," you might set the Display Name to "ABC Corporation - Purchasing" to distinguish it from another contact at the same company.

QuickBooks also lets you set up sub-customers (sometimes called "jobs") under a parent customer. This is useful if you do multiple projects for the same client and want to track each project's invoices and payments separately. To create a sub-customer, check the **"Is sub-customer"** checkbox and select the parent customer from the dropdown.

## Merging Duplicates and Making Customers Inactive

**Merging duplicate customers:**

If you accidentally created two records for the same customer (e.g., "ABC Corp" and "ABC Corporation"), you can merge them. All transactions will transfer to the record you keep.

1. Open the customer record you want to **get rid of** (the duplicate).
2. Change the **Customer Display Name** to exactly match the name of the customer you want to **keep**.
3. Click **Save**.
4. QuickBooks will ask if you want to merge — click **Yes**.

Warning: Merging cannot be undone. Double-check before you confirm.

**Making a customer inactive:**

When you stop doing business with a customer, don't delete them — make them inactive instead. This preserves all their historical transactions.

1. Go to **Sales > Customers**.
2. Find the customer, click their name to open the record.
3. Click the **Edit** button.
4. At the bottom of the form, click **Make Inactive**.
5. Confirm when prompted.

Inactive customers are hidden from your customer list by default but still appear in historical reports. You can view them by clicking **Settings** (gear icon) on the customer list and turning on "Include inactive."

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Entering a customer twice** with slightly different names — always search before adding a new customer.
- **Skipping the email address** — you won't be able to email invoices without it.
- **Using the Opening Balance field incorrectly** — only use it if you're not entering historical invoices; otherwise you'll double-count the balance.
- **Deleting customers instead of making them inactive** — QuickBooks won't let you delete customers with transactions, but making them inactive is cleaner anyway.
- **Inconsistent Display Name formatting** — mixing "First Last" and "Company Name" styles makes reports confusing.

## Key Takeaways

- A clean customer list is the foundation of accurate accounts receivable — invest time upfront to set it up correctly.
- The Customer Display Name controls how the customer appears on invoices and reports; keep the format consistent.
- Always search for an existing customer before adding a new one to avoid duplicates.
- Use "Make Inactive" instead of deleting customers to preserve transaction history.
- Opening Balance should only be used during initial setup for pre-existing balances — not alongside historical invoice entry.
