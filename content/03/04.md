# Creating and Sending Invoices

An invoice is the formal request for payment that tells your customer what they owe and when it's due — and tells QuickBooks to record that money as income.

## What You'll Learn

- How to create an invoice from scratch using the correct navigation path
- How to set payment terms and due dates
- How to add products, services, discounts, and sales tax to an invoice
- How to send an invoice by email directly from QuickBooks
- How to customize your invoice template and set up recurring invoices

---

## The Invoice Workflow

When you create and save an invoice in QuickBooks, two things happen at once. First, your customer gets a professional document telling them what they owe and when. Second, QuickBooks records the transaction in your books: it debits Accounts Receivable (meaning the customer owes you money) and credits your income account (meaning you've earned revenue).

That's why it matters to create invoices in QuickBooks rather than in Word or a separate tool — only a QuickBooks invoice updates your books automatically.

## Creating an Invoice Step by Step

**Navigation:** Click **+ New** (top left) > under the **Customers** column, click **Invoice**.

1. Click the **+ New** button in the top left corner.
2. Under the **Customers** column, select **Invoice**.
3. In the **Customer** field, start typing the customer's name and select from the dropdown. QuickBooks will auto-fill their billing address and any default payment terms you've set on their profile.
4. Confirm the **Invoice Date** — this is the date the invoice is issued and the date QuickBooks records the revenue.
5. Check the **Due Date**. If you have payment terms set on the customer profile, QuickBooks will calculate the due date automatically (e.g., Net 30 = 30 days from invoice date). You can also select terms from the **Terms** dropdown or set a specific due date manually.
6. In the **Product/Service** column, click the first line and search for the item you're invoicing for. Select it from the list.
7. Adjust the **Description** if needed — the default description from the item record will pre-fill.
8. Enter the **Quantity** and verify the **Rate**. The **Amount** calculates automatically.
9. Add additional line items on subsequent rows for each product or service.
10. If you need to add a discount, click the **Discount** field (you may need to enable discounts in Settings > Account and Settings > Sales > turn on Discount). Enter a percentage or dollar amount.
11. Review the **Sales Tax** section. If you've set up sales tax in QuickBooks, the applicable tax will calculate automatically based on the customer's location and the item's tax category.
12. Add a **Message on Invoice** — a short customer-facing note such as "Thank you for your business! Payment is due by [date]."
13. Add an internal **Memo** if needed — this is visible to you but not the customer.
14. Click **Save and Send** to email the invoice immediately, or **Save and Close** to save it for later.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Set default payment terms on each customer's profile rather than selecting them on every invoice. That way the due date calculates correctly every time without any extra steps.

## Payment Terms Explained

Payment terms control when an invoice is due. QuickBooks includes standard terms, and you can create custom ones.

| Term | What It Means |
|------|---------------|
| **Net 15** | Full payment due 15 days from invoice date |
| **Net 30** | Full payment due 30 days from invoice date |
| **Net 60** | Full payment due 60 days from invoice date |
| **Due on Receipt** | Payment expected immediately upon receiving the invoice |
| **1/10 Net 30** | 1% discount if paid within 10 days; full amount due in 30 days |

To add custom terms, go to **Settings > All Lists > Payment Terms > New**.

## Discounts and Sales Tax

**Adding a discount:**
Discounts can be applied as a percentage of the subtotal or as a fixed dollar amount. Enable this feature first at **Settings > Account and Settings > Sales > toggle on "Discount."** Once enabled, a Discount row appears on every invoice.

**Sales tax:**
If your business collects sales tax, QuickBooks Online has an automated sales tax engine. Once set up, it calculates the correct tax rate based on:
- Your business location
- The customer's location
- Whether each item is taxable

To mark an item as taxable, go to its product/service record and set the Tax category. To mark a customer as tax-exempt, go to the customer record > Tax info tab > check "This customer is tax exempt."

## Customizing and Sending Invoices

**Sending an invoice by email:**
When you click **Save and Send**, QuickBooks opens an email preview. You can edit the subject line, body, and recipient address before sending. The customer receives the invoice as a PDF attachment and a link to view it online and pay (if you have QuickBooks Payments enabled).

**Customizing your invoice template:**
Your invoices represent your business — a professional-looking template builds trust with customers.

1. Go to **Settings > Custom Form Styles**.
2. Click **Edit** on your existing template, or click **New Style** to create a new one.
3. Customize colors, logo, font, and which fields appear on the invoice.
4. On the **Content** tab, you can add or remove columns, move fields, and adjust what the customer sees.
5. Click **Done** to save.

## Setting Up Recurring Invoices

If you bill the same customer the same amount every month — retainer clients, subscriptions, monthly services — recurring invoices save you from creating the same invoice manually every billing cycle.

1. Create the invoice as normal with the correct customer, items, and amount.
2. Before saving, click **Make Recurring** at the bottom of the invoice.
3. Set the **Template Name** (e.g., "Metro Roofing Monthly Bookkeeping").
4. Choose the **Type:**
   - **Scheduled** — QuickBooks creates and sends the invoice automatically on the schedule you set.
   - **Reminder** — QuickBooks reminds you to review and send manually.
   - **Unscheduled** — saves as a template you can use anytime.
5. Set the **Interval** (e.g., Monthly) and **Start Date**.
6. Set how many times it should recur, or select "None" for ongoing.
7. Click **Save Template**.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Use Scheduled recurring invoices for clients with fixed monthly fees and Reminder type for clients whose invoice amount varies slightly each month (like hourly billing). That way the schedule is automated but you still review the amount before it goes out.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Not setting payment terms** — invoices without terms default to no due date, which makes your aging report meaningless.
- **Using the wrong invoice date** — the invoice date determines when revenue is recognized; don't backdate or future-date unless you have a specific reason.
- **Forgetting to send the invoice** — saving an invoice in QuickBooks doesn't automatically send it; always check whether the customer actually received it.
- **Adding items directly as line item text instead of using product/service records** — typed descriptions don't link to income accounts correctly.
- **Enabling recurring invoices on a Scheduled setting without double-checking the amount** — if rates change, update the recurring template or the wrong amount goes out automatically.

## Key Takeaways

- Every saved invoice updates accounts receivable and your income account — always use QuickBooks invoices rather than external documents.
- Payment terms drive the due date; set defaults on each customer profile to save time.
- QuickBooks automatically calculates sales tax when your items and customers are set up correctly.
- Custom invoice templates with your logo and brand colors project a professional image.
- Recurring invoices handle repetitive monthly billing automatically — use Scheduled for fixed amounts and Reminder for variable ones.
