# Products and Services Setup

The products and services list is the engine behind accurate invoicing — set it up right and every invoice you create will be faster, more consistent, and correctly categorized in your books.

## What You'll Learn

- Why setting up products and services correctly matters for invoicing and reporting
- The four types of items in QuickBooks and when to use each
- How to set up a service item step by step
- How to set up an inventory item with cost, price, and COGS account
- How to use bundles to package multiple items together

---

## Why Products and Services Matter

Every line item on an invoice or sales receipt links back to a product or service record. That record tells QuickBooks three things: what to charge, what income account to credit, and — for inventory — what the item costs and how many you have on hand.

If you skip this setup or create items on the fly with inconsistent naming, you end up with:

- Income scattered across wrong accounts
- Reports that show the same service listed five different ways
- Inventory counts that are never accurate
- Tax codes applied incorrectly

Spending 20 minutes building a solid products and services list pays off every time you send an invoice.

## The Four Item Types

QuickBooks Online offers four item types. Choosing the right one is important because each type behaves differently in your books.

**1. Service**
Used for any work you perform — consulting hours, labor, cleaning, design work. Service items have a rate (your price), an income account, and an optional description. They do not track quantity on hand.

**2. Non-inventory**
Used for physical goods you sell but don't track in inventory — for example, a contractor who buys materials specifically for a job and passes the cost through. Non-inventory items have a sales price and can optionally track a purchase cost.

**3. Inventory**
Used for physical products you stock and sell repeatedly. QuickBooks tracks how many you have on hand, the average cost, and automatically records Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) when you sell one. This type requires more setup but gives you powerful tracking.

**4. Bundle**
A collection of other items grouped together and sold as a package. Useful for service packages (e.g., "Starter Package = 3 hours consulting + setup fee") or product kits.

## Setting Up a Service Item

Service items are the most common type for small service businesses — consultants, contractors, agencies, and professionals.

**Navigation:** Go to **Settings** (gear icon, top right) > **Products and Services** > click **New** > select **Service**.

**Step-by-step:**

1. Click the **gear icon** (Settings) in the top right corner.
2. Under **Lists**, click **Products and Services**.
3. Click the **New** button.
4. Select **Service** from the item type options.
5. Enter a clear **Name** for the item (e.g., "Consulting - Hourly").
6. Optionally enter a **SKU** or item code if you use one.
7. Write a **Description** — this will auto-populate on invoices, so write it the way you want customers to see it (e.g., "Professional consulting services — billed per hour").
8. Enter the **Sales Price/Rate** (e.g., 150.00 for $150/hour). Leave blank if the rate varies per job.
9. Select the **Income Account** — this is where revenue from this service will be recorded (e.g., "Consulting Revenue" or "Services Income").
10. Set the **Sales Tax** category if applicable.
11. Click **Save and Close**.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** If your rates vary by client or project, leave the Sales Price field blank. QuickBooks will let you type in the price each time you add the item to an invoice, which is more flexible than creating a separate item for every rate.

## Setting Up an Inventory Item

Inventory items require a few extra steps because QuickBooks needs to know the purchase cost, the sales price, and which accounts to use for both the asset and the expense side.

**Navigation:** Go to **Settings** > **Products and Services** > **New** > select **Inventory**.

**Key fields for inventory items:**

- **Name:** The product name as it appears on invoices and reports.
- **Initial Quantity on Hand:** How many units you currently have in stock. Enter this only during initial setup.
- **As of Date:** The date your inventory count is accurate as of.
- **Inventory Asset Account:** This is a balance sheet account (usually "Inventory Asset") that holds the value of your stock.
- **Cost:** What you pay your supplier per unit. QuickBooks uses this to calculate the value of your inventory and to record COGS when you sell.
- **COGS Account:** The expense account that gets charged when you sell a unit (typically "Cost of Goods Sold").
- **Sales Price:** What you charge customers.
- **Income Account:** Where the revenue is recorded when you sell (e.g., "Product Sales").

**Step-by-step:**

1. Go to **Settings > Products and Services > New > Inventory**.
2. Enter the product **Name** (e.g., "Air Filter - 16x20").
3. Enter your current **Quantity on Hand** and the **As of Date**.
4. Confirm the **Inventory Asset Account** (default is "Inventory Asset").
5. Enter the **Cost** — what you pay per unit (e.g., 8.50).
6. Confirm the **COGS Account** (default is "Cost of Goods Sold").
7. Enter the **Sales Price** — what you charge customers (e.g., 18.00).
8. Select the **Income Account** (e.g., "Product Sales" or "Sales of Product Income").
9. Set **Low Stock Alert** quantity if you want QuickBooks to warn you when stock runs low.
10. Click **Save and Close**.

## Using Bundles to Package Items

Bundles let you group multiple products or services into a single line item on an invoice. The customer sees one line (the bundle name), while QuickBooks tracks each component separately behind the scenes.

**Example:** A "Website Launch Package" might bundle:
- 10 hours of design (Service)
- 1 domain setup (Service)
- Hosting setup fee (Service)

**To create a bundle:**

1. Go to **Settings > Products and Services > New > Bundle**.
2. Give the bundle a **Name** and **Description**.
3. Click **Add Product/Service** and search for each item to include.
4. Enter the **Quantity** of each item in the bundle.
5. Decide whether to **Display bundle components on invoice** — if checked, customers see each line item; if unchecked, they see only the bundle name and total.
6. Click **Save and Close**.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Bundles do not have their own price — the total is the sum of each component's price. If you want a flat package price that doesn't add up from parts, use a single Service item instead and price it at the package rate.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Using Non-inventory instead of Inventory** for products you stock — you'll lose quantity tracking and your COGS won't calculate correctly.
- **Assigning the wrong income account** — if all your services post to one generic account, you lose visibility into which services generate the most revenue.
- **Leaving the Description blank** — every time you invoice that item, you'll have to type the description manually.
- **Not setting a COGS account on inventory items** — QuickBooks may default to an incorrect account; always verify it.
- **Creating duplicate items** with slightly different names (e.g., "Consulting," "Consulting Hourly," "Consulting Fee") — standardize early and merge duplicates.

Set up the following two items in your QuickBooks company file:

**Item 1 - Service:**

## Key Takeaways

- The right item type — Service, Non-inventory, Inventory, or Bundle — controls how QuickBooks handles costs, quantities, and accounting entries.
- Always assign a specific income account to each item so your revenue reports show meaningful breakdowns.
- Inventory items need both a cost (what you pay) and a price (what you charge), plus a COGS account to accurately track profit.
- Writing good default descriptions on items saves time on every invoice you create.
- Bundles are powerful for packaging services but don't carry their own price — the total is always the sum of the parts.
