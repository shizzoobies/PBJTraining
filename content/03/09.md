## What Is Accounts Receivable Aging?

Accounts receivable (AR) aging is a snapshot of all the money your customers currently owe you, sorted by how long each invoice has been outstanding. The older a receivable is, the harder it typically is to collect — which makes this report one of the most important tools for managing cash flow.

Every business that sends invoices needs to review AR aging regularly. Without it, overdue accounts hide in the background while you're busy doing the work — and suddenly you realize a major client hasn't paid in four months.

The report divides outstanding invoices into time buckets:

- **Current (0-30 days):** Invoices within their normal payment window
- **31-60 days:** Slightly overdue — time for a friendly reminder
- **61-90 days:** Significantly overdue — requires direct follow-up
- **91+ days:** Seriously overdue — at risk of becoming uncollectable

## Running the AR Aging Reports

QuickBooks offers two versions of the AR Aging report. Use both depending on what you need.

**AR Aging Summary** — Shows one line per customer with their total balance broken into aging buckets. Best for a quick overview of your overall AR situation.

**AR Aging Detail** — Shows every individual open invoice, with its original amount, balance due, and which aging bucket it falls in. Best when you need to see exactly which invoices are overdue for each customer.

**To run the AR Aging Summary:**

1. Click **Reports** in the left navigation menu.
2. In the search bar, type "Aging" and select **Accounts Receivable Aging Summary**.
3. Set the **Report Date** to today (or the date you want the snapshot as of).
4. Click **Run Report**.
5. Review the columns: Customer name | Current | 1-30 | 31-60 | 61-90 | 91 and over | Total.

**To run the AR Aging Detail:**

1. Click **Reports** in the left navigation.
2. Search for "Aging" and select **Accounts Receivable Aging Detail**.
3. Set the report date and click **Run Report**.
4. Each row shows: Customer | Invoice # | Invoice Date | Due Date | Past Due Days | Amount | Open Balance.
5. Click any invoice number to open that invoice directly from the report.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Run your AR Aging report every Friday morning as part of a weekly routine. It takes two minutes and ensures overdue accounts don't slip past 60 days before you notice. The longer a receivable ages, the lower the probability of collection — industry data suggests accounts past 90 days have less than a 70% collection rate.

## Reading the Aging Report

Here's how to interpret what you see:

| Column | What It Means | Action |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| **Current** | Invoice is within payment terms, not yet due | No action needed |
| **1-30 days** | Invoice is 1-30 days past due | Send a polite reminder |
| **31-60 days** | Invoice is 31-60 days past due | Follow up directly — email and phone |
| **61-90 days** | Invoice is 61-90 days past due | Escalate — consider pausing future work |
| **91+ days** | Invoice is more than 91 days past due | Collections action or bad debt write-off |

When reviewing the report, look for:

- **Customers with large balances in the 61-90 or 91+ columns** — these need immediate personal contact
- **Customers who appear in multiple aging columns** — this signals a pattern of late payment, not a one-time oversight
- **Invoices you don't recognize** — these may be duplicates or errors that need investigation

## Sending Invoice Reminders

QuickBooks lets you send payment reminders directly from the platform — either manually or automatically.

**Sending a manual reminder:**

1. Go to **Sales > Invoices** (or run the AR Aging Detail report).
2. Find the overdue invoice.
3. Click the invoice to open it.
4. Click the dropdown arrow next to **Save and Send** and select **Send Reminder**.
5. QuickBooks opens an email with a pre-written reminder message and attaches the original invoice as a PDF.
6. Edit the subject line or message as needed — a personal tone works better than a generic template.
7. Click **Send**.

**Setting up automatic payment reminders:**

1. Go to **Settings** (gear icon) > **Account and Settings**.
2. Click the **Sales** tab.
3. Scroll to **Reminders** and click to expand.
4. Toggle on **Automatic invoice reminders**.
5. Configure up to three automatic reminders:
   - Set each reminder to trigger a specific number of days **before** or **after** the due date.
   - Customize the subject line and message body for each reminder.
   - Example setup: Reminder 1 = 3 days before due date ("Just a heads-up — your invoice is due soon"), Reminder 2 = 1 day after due date ("Your invoice is now past due"), Reminder 3 = 14 days after due date ("Second notice — please contact us").
6. Click **Save**.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Customize your reminder messages to match your business's tone. A warm, personal reminder gets a better response than a cold, automated-sounding one. Use the customer's name, reference the specific invoice number, and offer an easy way to pay (include a payment link if you have QuickBooks Payments).

## Writing Off Bad Debt

When you've exhausted collection efforts and a customer simply isn't going to pay, you need to write off the bad debt. This removes the invoice from your AR balance and records the loss as an expense — so your books reflect reality.

**Setup required:** You need a "Bad Debt" expense account and a "Bad Debt" service item.

**One-time setup:**

1. Go to **Settings > Chart of Accounts > New**.
2. Create an expense account called **Bad Debt** (Account Type: Expenses, Detail Type: Bad Debts).
3. Go to **Settings > Products and Services > New > Service**.
4. Name it **Bad Debt Write-off**.
5. Set the **Income Account** to your new Bad Debt expense account.
6. Leave the price at $0.00 (you'll enter the amount on the credit memo).
7. Make sure it is marked as **non-taxable**.

**Writing off the bad debt:**

1. Go to **+ New > Credit Memo**.
2. Select the customer whose debt you are writing off.
3. In the **Product/Service** field, select **Bad Debt Write-off**.
4. Enter the amount of the unpaid invoice as the credit amount.
5. In the **Message** field, note the invoice number(s) being written off.
6. Click **Save and Close**.
7. When prompted, apply the credit to the open invoice — this closes the invoice and records the loss.

The bad debt expense flows to your Profit & Loss report as an expense, reducing net income appropriately. The customer's AR balance is cleared.

## Collections Best Practices

Strong AR management starts before an invoice is ever overdue. Follow these practices to minimize bad debt:

**Before work begins:**
- Get a signed agreement or accepted estimate for every job
- Set clear payment terms and communicate them upfront
- For new customers, consider requiring a deposit (50% upfront is common)
- Run a credit check for large new clients if your industry warrants it

**During the billing cycle:**
- Send invoices immediately after completing work — delays in invoicing signal to customers that payment is not urgent
- Follow up on day 1 past due, not day 30 — early contact normalizes prompt payment
- Keep a collections log noting every contact attempt and customer response

**For chronic late payers:**
- Consider requiring prepayment or shorter terms (Net 15 instead of Net 30)
- Pause new work until the outstanding balance is settled
- Add a late payment fee (check your state's legal limits) — even if you don't enforce it every time, the policy sets expectations

**For serious delinquencies:**
- Send a formal demand letter via certified mail
- Engage a collections agency for amounts that justify the cost
- Consult an attorney for amounts over $5,000–$10,000
- Consider small claims court for amounts within your state's limit

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Never reviewing the AR Aging report** — overdue accounts grow invisibly until they become uncollectable.
- **Waiting too long to follow up** — a 30-day-old invoice is much easier to collect than a 90-day-old one; act early.
- **Writing off bad debt by deleting the invoice** — this removes the revenue from your books entirely; the correct method (credit memo to Bad Debt account) keeps the history and records the true loss.
- **Sending only automated reminders without personal follow-up** — automated emails are often ignored; a phone call to a 60+ day account is significantly more effective.
- **Not setting up automatic reminders** when QuickBooks makes it easy and free — this is low-effort prevention that catches many late payments before they become problems.

## Key Takeaways

- The AR Aging report sorts all outstanding invoices by how long they've been overdue — review it weekly to catch problems early.
- The aging columns (Current, 1-30, 31-60, 61-90, 91+) tell you both the amount owed and how urgently you need to act.
- Automatic payment reminders in QuickBooks are easy to set up and catch many late payments without any manual effort.
- Write off bad debt using a credit memo linked to a Bad Debt expense account — never by deleting the original invoice.
- Strong AR management starts before work begins: clear terms, signed agreements, and immediate invoicing minimize how often you have to chase payments at all.
