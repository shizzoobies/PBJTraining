# Setting Up Direct Deposit

Direct deposit is the most reliable and professional way to pay your employees — it eliminates lost checks, reduces administrative work, and gets money into employee accounts on time every payroll.

## What You'll Learn
- What you need in place before direct deposit can go live
- How to enter employee bank account information in QuickBooks
- What the prenote period is and why it exists
- What to expect during the first direct deposit payroll run
- How to handle failed direct deposits and fall back to paper checks

---

## Requirements for Direct Deposit

Before you can send a single dollar via direct deposit, three things must be in place:

**1. A verified company bank account.** QuickBooks must have your business checking account linked and verified through the micro-deposit process (covered in Lesson 5.1). This account is the funding source for all direct deposits. Without verification, QuickBooks will not transmit payroll funds.

**2. Payroll subscription with direct deposit enabled.** All QuickBooks Payroll tiers include direct deposit, but the processing speed differs:
- **Core**: Next-day direct deposit (submit payroll one banking day before payday)
- **Premium**: Same-day direct deposit (submit payroll by 7:00 AM PT on payday)
- **Elite**: Same-day direct deposit with the same cutoff as Premium

**3. Lead time before the first run.** The very first time you run direct deposit — even with everything verified — QuickBooks processes a prenote on the employee's bank account. Plan for the first paycheck to be a paper check, or run direct deposit at least one payroll cycle before you need it to be live.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Set a recurring calendar reminder 2 business days before each payday (or the day before for same-day plans) as your payroll submission deadline. Missing the cutoff means employees do not get paid on time — even if the payroll is submitted that afternoon.

## Entering Employee Bank Information

Each employee who wants direct deposit must provide their bank account information. The standard form for this is a direct deposit authorization form, which you should keep on file. Many payroll providers, including Intuit, offer a self-service option where employees enter their own information through the QuickBooks Workforce portal.

**Option 1: Enter bank info manually in the employee profile**

1. Go to **Payroll > Employees** and click the employee's name.
2. Click the **Direct Deposit** tab (or scroll to the Direct Deposit section if using the single-page layout).
3. Toggle **Use Direct Deposit** to on.
4. Click **Add bank account**.
5. Enter the employee's bank **routing number** — 9 digits, found in the bottom-left corner of a personal check or in the employee's online banking portal.
6. Enter the employee's **account number** — found to the right of the routing number on a check. Account numbers vary in length (typically 8–12 digits).
7. Select the **account type**: Checking or Savings.
8. Click **Done**.

**Option 2: Invite employees to enter their own bank info via QuickBooks Workforce**

1. Go to **Payroll > Employees** and click the employee's name.
2. Scroll to the **QuickBooks Workforce** section and click **Invite to QuickBooks Workforce**.
3. Enter the employee's email address and click **Send Invite**.
4. The employee receives an email, creates a Workforce account, and enters their own bank details securely.
5. Once the employee submits, their banking information is saved to their profile and visible to you in QuickBooks.

The Workforce method is preferred for larger teams — it reduces data entry errors on your end and puts responsibility for accuracy on the employee.

## Splitting Direct Deposit Across Multiple Accounts

Some employees want their paycheck split between accounts — for example, directing a fixed amount to savings each pay period and the remainder to checking. QuickBooks supports up to two bank accounts per employee.

1. After setting up the first account, click **Add another bank account** in the Direct Deposit section.
2. Enter the routing and account number for the second account.
3. Set the **split type** for the first account:
   - **Fixed dollar amount**: A specific dollar figure goes to this account each pay period (e.g., $200 to savings).
   - **Percentage**: A percentage of net pay goes to this account (e.g., 20% to savings).
   - **Remainder**: Whatever is left after other splits goes here (typically used for the primary checking account).
4. Set the second account to **Remainder** if the first account uses a fixed amount or percentage.
5. Click **Done**.

## The Prenote Period

A prenote (short for prenotification) is a zero-dollar test transaction that the ACH network sends to an employee's bank before the first real direct deposit. Its purpose is to verify that the routing number and account number are valid and active before real money is transmitted.

Here is what happens during the prenote process:

1. You enter the employee's bank information in QuickBooks and save it.
2. On the next payroll run, QuickBooks transmits a $0.00 prenote to the employee's bank.
3. The prenote takes one to two banking days to process.
4. If the bank accepts the prenote (account number is valid and active), the account is cleared for live deposits.
5. If the bank rejects the prenote (wrong account number, closed account, etc.), QuickBooks notifies you and the employee is flagged — you must collect corrected information and resubmit.

During the prenote period, the employee's paycheck is issued as a **paper check** unless you have Premium or Elite same-day direct deposit, in which case QuickBooks may attempt live transmission after a shorter verification window.

The prenote is completely invisible to the employee — no money moves, and nothing appears in their bank statement. It is a behind-the-scenes verification step.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Add direct deposit information for new employees on their first day, well before their first payday. This gives the prenote time to clear so their very first paycheck can be a direct deposit rather than a paper check.

## Running the First Direct Deposit Payroll

Once the prenote has cleared for an employee, the next payroll run will automatically send their pay via direct deposit. No extra steps are required on your end.

During the payroll run process:

1. Run payroll as normal (covered in Lesson 5.3).
2. On the Preview Payroll screen, employees with active direct deposit show **Direct Deposit** under their payment method. Employees still in the prenote period show **Check**.
3. Submit the payroll.
4. QuickBooks transmits an ACH file to your bank, which forwards it to each employee's bank.
5. Funds are debited from your business bank account on the transmission day and credited to employee accounts on the pay date.

Employees receive a notification (email or push notification through the Workforce app) when their direct deposit is processed. They can view their pay stub through QuickBooks Workforce.

## Handling Failed Direct Deposits

Direct deposit failures do happen — an employee closes an account without telling you, enters a wrong digit, or their bank rejects the transaction. Here is how to handle them:

1. QuickBooks notifies you by email when a direct deposit is returned by the bank. The notification includes the employee name and the reason code for the failure.
2. The returned funds are credited back to your company bank account, typically within one to two banking days.
3. Go to **Payroll > Employees**, click the affected employee, and navigate to their **Direct Deposit** tab.
4. Remove the failed bank account and enter the corrected information (or send the employee a new Workforce invite to update it themselves).
5. Issue the employee a paper check for the failed payroll period — QuickBooks allows you to print a check from the original payroll run without rerunning payroll.
6. The corrected bank account will go through the prenote process before the next live direct deposit.

Common reasons for direct deposit failure:
- Account closed
- Account number entered incorrectly
- Routing number entered incorrectly (especially for credit unions, which sometimes have multiple routing numbers)
- Account does not accept ACH credits (rare, but some savings accounts have restrictions)

## Paper Check Fallback

Employees who do not set up direct deposit, or whose direct deposit has failed, receive paper checks. QuickBooks can print checks directly if you use QuickBooks-compatible check stock, or you can print a pay stub and write a manual check.

To print checks in QuickBooks:
1. After submitting payroll, click **Print Checks** from the payroll confirmation screen.
2. Select the employees who need paper checks.
3. Load check stock into your printer (QuickBooks sells pre-printed check stock at intuit.com, or you can use blank check stock with MICR ink).
4. Click **Print**.

You can also reprint checks later by going to **Payroll > Employees**, selecting the employee, and finding the paycheck under their payment history.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Not collecting a signed direct deposit authorization form.** This is your legal documentation that the employee consented to electronic payment. Keep it on file even if the employee entered their information through Workforce.
- **Entering the account number from a deposit slip instead of a check.** Deposit slip account numbers are sometimes formatted differently than what the bank needs for ACH — always use the number from the bottom of a personal check.
- **Forgetting to account for the prenote period when hiring a new employee.** If someone starts Monday and payday is Friday, direct deposit will not be live for that first check. Plan to issue a paper check.
- **Ignoring returned deposit notifications.** QuickBooks sends an email when a deposit fails. If you ignore it, the employee may not know their pay did not arrive and you may face a delay in correcting it.
- **Using the wrong routing number for savings accounts.** Some banks use different routing numbers for ACH versus wire transfers. Confirm the ACH routing number with the employee before entry.

## Key Takeaways

- Direct deposit requires a verified company bank account, an active payroll subscription, and employee bank information — all three must be in place before any funds can be transmitted.
- The prenote period is a one-time zero-dollar test transaction that verifies an employee's bank account before real deposits are sent; expect paper checks for the first pay period.
- QuickBooks supports splitting direct deposit across two accounts per employee using fixed dollar amounts, percentages, or a remainder designation.
- Failed direct deposits return funds to your account within one to two banking days — you must collect corrected bank information and reissue the payment manually.
- The QuickBooks Workforce portal allows employees to enter and update their own bank information, reducing data entry errors and keeping authorization on file electronically.
