# Setting Up Payroll in QuickBooks

Getting payroll set up correctly from the start saves you from costly errors, tax penalties, and employee frustration down the road.

## What You'll Learn
- The differences between QuickBooks Payroll subscription tiers and which one fits your business
- How to activate payroll and connect your company's tax information
- How to configure pay schedules that match how your business pays employees
- How to link your bank account and set up e-file and e-pay with the IRS and your state
- What the initial setup checklist covers so nothing gets missed

---

## Choosing the Right Payroll Subscription Tier

QuickBooks Online offers three payroll tiers. Picking the right one upfront matters because upgrading mid-year is possible but can disrupt workflows.

**QuickBooks Payroll Core** is the entry-level option. It handles automated payroll runs, next-day direct deposit, automatic tax calculations, and e-filing of federal and state payroll tax forms. This tier suits small businesses with straightforward pay structures and no HR needs.

**QuickBooks Payroll Premium** adds same-day direct deposit, an HR support center, expert review of your payroll setup, and worker's compensation administration. It also includes a time tracking integration with QuickBooks Time (formerly TSheets). Premium works well for businesses with hourly workers who clock in and out.

**QuickBooks Payroll Elite** is the top tier. It includes everything in Premium plus tax penalty protection (Intuit will pay penalties and interest caused by its own calculation errors, up to $25,000 per year), 24/7 expert support, and a personal HR advisor. Elite is worth the cost for businesses with complex payroll, multistate employees, or those who cannot afford a payroll mistake.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** If you run payroll for five or fewer employees with straightforward salaries or hourly rates, Core is usually sufficient. Add Premium once you need time tracking integration or HR support documents.

## Activating Payroll and Entering Company Tax Information

Once you've subscribed to a payroll tier, QuickBooks walks you through an activation wizard. Here is the full setup sequence:

1. Log in to QuickBooks Online and click **Payroll** in the left navigation menu.
2. Click **Get Started** on the payroll activation screen.
3. Enter your **Employer Identification Number (EIN)**. This is the nine-digit number assigned by the IRS to your business. It appears on your IRS confirmation letter (CP 575) and any previously filed 941 forms. Do not use your personal Social Security Number here even if you are a sole proprietor — if you do not have an EIN, apply for one free at IRS.gov before continuing.
4. Enter your **business legal name** exactly as it appears on your EIN confirmation letter. Even minor spelling differences can cause e-filing mismatches.
5. Select your **business type** (LLC, S-Corp, C-Corp, sole proprietor, etc.). This affects which tax forms QuickBooks will generate.
6. Enter your **federal deposit schedule**. If you are a new employer or deposited less than $50,000 in payroll taxes during the lookback period, you are a monthly depositor. If you deposited $50,000 or more, you are a semi-weekly depositor. If you're unsure, check with your accountant.
7. Enter your **state tax information**: state Employer Account Number, state unemployment insurance (SUI) rate, and any local tax IDs that apply. Each state has a different format — QuickBooks prompts you for the right fields based on your state.
8. Click **Save and Continue**.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Your state SUI rate changes annually and is mailed to you by your state's labor department each December or January. Keep that letter. If you enter the wrong rate, you will over- or under-remit state unemployment taxes all year.

## Configuring Pay Schedules

A pay schedule tells QuickBooks how often you pay employees and when those payments are due. You can create multiple pay schedules if different employee groups are paid on different cadences (for example, hourly workers paid weekly and salaried managers paid biweekly).

The four standard pay schedules are:

- **Weekly** — 52 pay periods per year. Common for hourly and construction workers.
- **Biweekly** — 26 pay periods per year. The most common schedule for U.S. small businesses.
- **Semimonthly** — 24 pay periods per year (typically the 1st and 15th of each month). Common for salaried office workers.
- **Monthly** — 12 pay periods per year. Less common; sometimes used for owner compensation or contracted employees.

To create a pay schedule:
1. Go to **Payroll > Employees**.
2. Click the **Payroll Settings** gear icon (top right of the Employees page).
3. Select **Pay Schedules** from the settings menu.
4. Click **Add Pay Schedule**.
5. Name the schedule (e.g., "Biweekly Hourly" or "Monthly Salary").
6. Choose the pay frequency, select the next pay date, and choose the pay period end date.
7. Click **OK** to save.

## Linking Your Bank Account and Setting Up E-Pay

QuickBooks uses your bank account to fund direct deposit payrolls and to remit payroll taxes electronically. This step requires micro-deposit verification, so plan two to three business days before your first payroll.

1. Go to **Payroll > Overview**.
2. Click **Set Up Direct Deposit** (or **Link Bank Account** if direct deposit was already enabled at the subscription level).
3. Enter your bank **routing number** and **checking account number**. Use a dedicated payroll checking account if possible — it makes reconciliation cleaner and protects your operating funds if there is a payroll error.
4. QuickBooks sends two small test deposits to your account (typically under $1.00 each) within one to two business days.
5. Log in to your bank account, find the two deposits, and note the exact amounts.
6. Return to **Payroll > Overview > Verify Bank Account** and enter the two deposit amounts.
7. Once verified, click **Activate E-Pay** to authorize QuickBooks to pull funds for direct deposits and tax payments on your behalf.

For e-filing setup, navigate to **Payroll > Payroll Tax > E-file and E-pay Enrollment** and follow the prompts for each federal and state jurisdiction where you have tax obligations.

## Initial Setup Checklist

Before running your first payroll, confirm each item below is complete:

- EIN entered and matches IRS records
- Business type and legal name confirmed
- Federal deposit schedule selected (monthly or semi-weekly)
- State employer account number and SUI rate entered for each state
- At least one pay schedule created
- Bank account linked and micro-deposit verification completed
- E-pay enrollment completed for federal (941/940) taxes
- E-file enrollment completed for applicable state forms
- All employees added (covered in Lesson 5.2)
- Year-to-date payroll history entered if you are setting up mid-year

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Skipping the bank verification step.** Some business owners start adding employees before their bank account is verified. Direct deposit will not work, and QuickBooks may hold your first payroll run until verification is complete.
- **Entering the wrong SUI rate.** Your state unemployment rate is specific to your business based on claims history. Do not use the new-employer rate if you have been in business more than a year — check your state's official notice.
- **Mismatching the business name with the EIN.** Even a missing comma or "LLC" can cause e-filed forms to reject. Copy the name character-for-character from your EIN letter.
- **Not setting up e-pay before the first payroll.** If e-pay is not enabled, QuickBooks will calculate the taxes but will not deposit them — leaving you responsible for manually remitting to the IRS by the due date.
- **Using a personal bank account for payroll.** Mixing payroll funds with personal funds creates bookkeeping nightmares and can complicate tax filings.

## Key Takeaways

- QuickBooks Payroll comes in three tiers (Core, Premium, Elite) — choose based on your deposit speed needs, HR requirements, and risk tolerance.
- Your EIN and business legal name must match IRS records exactly to avoid e-filing rejections.
- Pay schedules must be configured before you can assign employees to them — set these up first.
- Bank account verification via micro-deposits takes one to two business days; plan accordingly before your first payroll run.
- E-pay and e-file enrollments are separate steps that must both be completed to fully automate your payroll tax compliance.
