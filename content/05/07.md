# Year-End: W-2s and 1099s

Year-end payroll filing is one of the most deadline-driven tasks in small business accounting — knowing the steps, the deadlines, and the correction process keeps you compliant and out of IRS trouble.

## What You'll Learn
- The difference between a W-2 and a 1099, and which workers receive which form
- How to complete QuickBooks year-end tasks before filing
- How to verify employee information to prevent filing errors
- How to run, review, and e-file W-2s directly from QuickBooks
- How to correct a W-2 error after filing using Form W-2c

---

## W-2 vs. 1099: Which Form for Which Worker

The form a worker receives depends entirely on how they are classified — as an employee or as an independent contractor. Misclassification is a serious IRS audit trigger and can result in back taxes, penalties, and interest.

**Form W-2** is issued to employees — anyone you pay through payroll, from whose wages you withhold income tax and FICA. W-2s report:
- Total wages paid during the year
- Federal income tax withheld
- Social Security and Medicare wages and taxes
- State wages and state income tax withheld
- Pre-tax benefits (health insurance, 401k contributions shown in Box 12)

Every employee who was paid any amount during the tax year must receive a W-2, even if they only worked one day.

**Form 1099-NEC** (Nonemployee Compensation) is issued to independent contractors — individuals you paid $600 or more during the year for services but who are not employees. You do not withhold taxes from their pay. Contractors are responsible for their own self-employment taxes. 1099-NEC forms are filed with the IRS and sent to the contractor.

**Important distinction:** QuickBooks Payroll handles W-2s automatically through the payroll system. 1099s for contractors are handled separately through the **Vendors** or **Expenses** section of QuickBooks, not through Payroll. This lesson focuses on W-2s for payroll employees.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** If you are unsure whether a worker is an employee or an independent contractor, default to treating them as an employee. Misclassifying an employee as a contractor shifts payroll tax liability to you — plus penalties. The IRS has a 20-factor test for worker classification; consult your accountant if you are on the borderline.

## Year-End Tasks Checklist

Before generating W-2s, work through this checklist to ensure your data is clean. A W-2 filed with wrong information requires a W-2c correction — an extra filing that delays year-end close for you and creates confusion for your employee.

Complete these tasks before January 1 (or as early in January as possible):

- Verify that all payrolls for the year have been submitted and posted in QuickBooks — no pending or draft payrolls should remain.
- Confirm that all payroll tax payments have been made and that your Payroll Tax Liability report shows a zero balance for the prior year.
- Review each employee's legal name and Social Security Number against the original documents on file.
- Verify current mailing addresses for all employees — W-2s are mailed to the address on file if paper copies are required.
- Confirm that all pre-tax benefit amounts (health insurance, FSA, 401k) are correctly recorded — these appear in specific boxes on the W-2.
- Review any employees who had changes during the year: pay rate increases, state changes, deduction changes, or mid-year starts and terminations.
- Ensure that any third-party sick pay (disability payments made by an insurer on your behalf) has been reported to QuickBooks if applicable.

QuickBooks provides a built-in year-end checklist accessible from **Payroll > Overview > Year-End Checklist**. This list is interactive — you can mark items complete as you go and it links directly to the relevant settings pages.

## Verifying Employee Information Before Filing

Running the **Employee Details** report (covered in Lesson 5.6) is the most efficient way to spot errors before filing. For W-2 accuracy, pay particular attention to:

1. Go to **Reports > Employee Details** and run it for the full calendar year.
2. For each employee, verify:
   - **Legal name** matches their Social Security card exactly — even the order of first and middle names.
   - **SSN** is correct and complete (9 digits, no transpositions).
   - **Home address** is current as of December 31 of the tax year — this is the address that appears on the W-2.
   - **Year-to-date gross wages** make sense given their pay rate and number of pay periods worked.
3. If you find an error in a name or SSN, correct it in the employee profile immediately: **Payroll > Employees > [Employee Name] > Personal tab > Edit**.
4. After corrections, run the Employee Details report again to confirm the changes are reflected.

[IMAGE: QuickBooks Employee Details report showing year-to-date wage totals, name, SSN (masked), and address fields for verification]

The Social Security Administration rejects W-2 filings where the name and SSN do not match their records. Each rejected W-2 generates a notice and requires a correction — a process that can take weeks to resolve.

## Running W-2s in QuickBooks

Once your data is verified and all year-end checklist items are complete, you are ready to generate and file W-2s.

1. Go to **Payroll** in the left navigation menu.
2. Click **Payroll Tax** at the top of the Payroll section.
3. Click **Annual Forms**.
4. Under the **Federal** section, find **W-2, Copies A & D (Employer)** and click **File Now** (or **View** to review first).
5. QuickBooks generates a draft W-2 for every active and terminated employee who received wages during the year.
6. Review the W-2 summary page showing all employees, their total wages, and the tax amounts. Scroll through and verify totals for any employees whose numbers look unexpected.
7. If you need to review an individual W-2, click the employee's name to see a full form preview showing all boxes populated.
8. When you are satisfied the information is correct, click **Submit** to e-file with the Social Security Administration (SSA). QuickBooks files the employer copies (Copy A) directly with the SSA and the federal filing (W-3 transmittal) simultaneously.
9. QuickBooks confirms the submission and provides a confirmation number. Save or screenshot this confirmation.

## Distributing W-2s to Employees

After e-filing, employees must receive their copies by **January 31**. This is a federal deadline — late distribution can result in IRS penalties starting at $60 per form.

QuickBooks provides two distribution methods:

**Electronic delivery via QuickBooks Workforce:**
1. After e-filing, go to **Payroll > Employees**.
2. Click **W-2 Distribution** or navigate back to **Payroll Tax > Annual Forms**.
3. Select the option to deliver W-2s electronically to employees via QuickBooks Workforce.
4. Employees who are enrolled in Workforce receive an email notification and can log in to download and print their W-2 any time.
5. Employees must have consented to electronic delivery — QuickBooks prompts for this consent during Workforce enrollment. If consent was not obtained, you must provide paper copies.

**Paper mailing:**
1. From **Annual Forms**, select **W-2, Copy B, C, 2** (the employee copies).
2. Click **Print** and load standard printer paper or pre-printed W-2 forms.
3. Mail to the employee's address of record via first-class mail before January 31.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Encourage all employees to enroll in QuickBooks Workforce and consent to electronic W-2 delivery when they are hired — not in January when you are scrambling. Electronic delivery is faster, cheaper, and creates an instant delivery record.

## Correcting W-2 Errors: Form W-2c

If you discover an error on a W-2 after it has been filed — wrong SSN, wrong wages, wrong name — you must file a **W-2c** (Corrected Wage and Tax Statement) with the SSA and provide the employee with a corrected copy.

**How to file a W-2c in QuickBooks:**

1. Go to **Payroll > Payroll Tax > Annual Forms**.
2. Scroll past the regular W-2 forms to find **W-2c**.
3. Click **Create W-2c** for the affected employee.
4. Enter the **incorrect information** in the "Previously Reported" column and the **corrected information** in the "Correct Information" column. Only fill in boxes where a change is being made.
5. Review the form for accuracy — a W-2c error requires yet another correction, so be thorough.
6. Click **E-file** to submit the W-2c to the SSA.
7. Print and mail (or electronically deliver) a copy of the corrected W-2c to the employee.

The employee should attach the W-2c to their tax return or, if they have already filed, file an amended return (Form 1040-X).

**Important:** If the error involves an incorrect SSN, the SSA may require a paper filing with a cover letter rather than an electronic filing. Check IRS Publication 1141 for guidance on SSN corrections.

## The January 31 Deadline

January 31 is the unified deadline for all of the following:
- **Employees must receive their W-2 copies** (mailed or electronically delivered)
- **W-2s must be filed with the SSA** (Copy A, transmitted via QuickBooks e-file)
- **1099-NEC forms must be delivered to contractors** and filed with the IRS

Missing the January 31 deadline triggers IRS penalties:
- $60 per form filed 1–30 days late
- $120 per form filed 31 days late through August 1
- $310 per form filed after August 1 or not filed at all
- $630 per form if the failure is intentional

These penalties apply per form, per filing — a business with 20 employees that misses the deadline by two months faces potentially $2,400 in penalties ($120 x 20).

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Waiting until mid-January to start the verification process.** Employee corrections take time, especially if you cannot reach a former employee to confirm their SSN. Start the checklist in November or early December.
- **Assuming QuickBooks will auto-correct employee name errors.** If you entered a name wrong during employee setup, it stays wrong on the W-2. QuickBooks files what is in the system — it does not cross-reference SSA records.
- **Filing W-2s before reconciling all payroll tax payments.** If a tax liability remains open from earlier in the year, the W-2 amounts may not match your 941 filings — that inconsistency triggers IRS matching notices.
- **Sending W-2s to old addresses for terminated employees.** Update the address of terminated employees (even if they are inactive) before printing W-2s. If a W-2 is undeliverable by mail, you have an additional obligation to obtain a correct address.
- **Ignoring a W-2c because the error seems minor.** Even a small wage discrepancy between the W-2 and the employee's 941 records can generate an IRS matching notice. File the correction promptly.

## Key Takeaways

- W-2s go to employees paid through payroll; 1099-NECs go to independent contractors paid $600 or more — classification errors are a major audit risk.
- Start the year-end verification process in November or December, not January — correcting name and SSN errors before filing prevents W-2c filings.
- QuickBooks e-files W-2s directly to the Social Security Administration from **Payroll > Payroll Tax > Annual Forms**; the W-3 transmittal is generated and submitted automatically.
- The January 31 deadline applies to both employee delivery and SSA filing — penalties start at $60 per form and increase with time.
- W-2c corrections must be filed any time a W-2 contains wrong wages, a wrong SSN, or a wrong name — even after the employee has already filed their personal tax return.
